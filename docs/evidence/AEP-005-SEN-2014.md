## 2. Historical Context

Senegal's 2014–2019 period represents a different SSA-LIC trajectory than Zambia's across the same years. Where Zambia's vulnerability was concentrated in a single commodity (copper), Senegal's export base was more diversified — phosphates, groundnuts, fish, and remittances — but also subject to commodity price cycles. The CFA franc peg to the euro provided monetary stability absent in Zambia's floating kwacha, but also constrained exchange rate adjustment as a shock absorber.

The trigger was the global commodity price decline of 2014–2016, which reduced phosphate and groundnut export revenues. Senegal issued its first Eurobond in June 2014 (USD 500M), signalling fiscal confidence — but the bond was issued at the cusp of the commodity cycle downturn. The 2015–2016 trough combined revenue shortfall with donor conditionality pressures. Senegal's recovery from 2017 onward was driven by the Plan Sénégal Émergent (PSE) — a long-term structural investment program anchored by public infrastructure spending — and by early-stage oil and gas exploration signals (commercial discoveries in offshore fields announced 2014–2016, with production not starting until 2024).

By 2019, Senegal had stabilised at growth rates approaching 5–6%, a meaningfully better outcome than Zambia over the same period. This cross-entity comparison within the SSA-LIC family provides a useful within-family contrast: same calibration family, same period, different shock profile and recovery path.

## 7. Known Limitations

- [x] **SSA-LIC T3 multiplier:** Fosu (2011) cross-sectional SSA estimate; Senegal-specific calibration unavailable. DIRECTION_ONLY ceiling reflects this. The CFA franc peg and BCEAO monetary anchor are structural differences from typical SSA-LIC scenarios that are not captured in the fiscal multiplier range.
- [x] **CFA franc peg not modelled:** Senegal operates within the BCEAO monetary union, which provides a monetary anchor absent in most SSA-LIC countries (including Zambia). The engine does not model fixed exchange rate dynamics or BCEAO reserve pooling. This may cause the engine to overstate the exchange rate transmission channel relative to Senegal's actual experience.
- [x] **PSE investment program:** The Plan Sénégal Émergent is a long-term structural investment program that contributes to Senegal's recovery from Step 4 onward. The engine captures the fiscal impulse of increased public investment but does not model the structural productivity effects of PSE infrastructure investment. The recovery signal at Steps 4–6 is advisory.
- [x] **Extremely wide CI at Step 6:** [0.1360, 0.9522] — the magnitude of fin_composite at Step 6 is not interpretable. Six-step T3 uncertainty accumulation exceeds the useful range of the confidence interval. This is an inherent limitation of T3 calibrated multi-year scenarios.
- [x] **No failure modes detected:** Consistent with the scenario — Senegal did not experience systemic financial stress during this period. The absence of failure mode detection is directionally correct for this entry, unlike AEP-004 (ZMB) where failure mode non-detection despite pre-default trajectory is a known gap.

## 8. Plain-Language Synthesis

The engine finds that Senegal's 2014–2019 trajectory is marked by a moderate commodity-shock-driven decline (trough at Step 3, 2016) followed by partial recovery from Step 4 onward — directionally consistent with the documented trough-recovery arc driven by PSE infrastructure investment and oil sector signals. The fin_composite stabilises and partially recovers by Step 6; the hd_composite follows with a marginal uptick. Both trajectories are far less severe than ZMB over the same period, which is the correct within-family ordering.

The CI band at Step 6 becomes essentially uninformative (spanning 82% of the full composite range). This is a known limitation of six-step T3 calibrated scenarios; the directional trajectory is preserved but no specific Step 6 magnitude should be cited. A Senegalese finance ministry analyst using this entry can rely on the engine to show that Senegal's macro trajectory stayed meaningfully better than ZMB over this period, but should not cite the specific composite values as calibrated.
