## 2. Historical Context

The counter-factual departs from the historical record at the moment of programme negotiation in May 2010. In the historical path (documented in AEP-001-GRC-2010), Greece accepted a €110bn programme requiring 8% of GDP in immediate fiscal cuts. The structural context for the counter-factual hypothesis is drawn from the Blanchard–Leigh (2013) finding, published three years after the programme was agreed, that fiscal multipliers in the Eurozone were substantially higher than assumed during programme design — the IMF's 0.5 multiplier assumption was approximately 2.5× too low. Had this been known in 2010, a smaller, more gradual programme may have been considered.

The counter-factual posits that Greece negotiated a €70bn programme (approximately 30% of GDP) with a 4% of GDP cut at Step 1 rather than 8%, and lighter subsequent packages — 2.5% at Step 2 rather than 5%, and reduced consolidation targets through Step 4. Capital controls at Step 6 are not introduced in the counter-factual path, on the hypothesis that a less severe contraction avoided the 2015 bank-run dynamic.

There is no historical record of this programme. Greece's negotiating leverage in May 2010 was extremely limited — creditors held decisive power, and no lighter programme was on offer. The counter-factual is therefore a structured analytical exercise, not a reconstructable historical alternative. Its control inputs are designated INFERRED_STRUCTURAL (Tier 3): plausible in design but without empirical anchor.

The comparison question is whether the engine finds a meaningfully different trajectory on human development under the lighter path — not whether the lighter path was achievable.

---

## 3. Question Posed

**Question:** Does the engine find a measurably better human development trajectory under a smaller, more gradual fiscal adjustment programme (€70bn, 30% GDP, front-loaded cut of 4% vs 8% of GDP) than under the documented Troika path?

**Scenario type rationale:** This is a Type B counter-factual — the alternative policy path (lighter conditionality) diverges from the historical programme from Step 1 and was not historically executed. The counter-factual is the path in which a smaller IMF programme with gentler front-loaded fiscal cuts was implemented beginning in 2010.

## 6. Fidelity Assessment

**Entry-level fidelity ceiling:** `DIRECTION_ONLY`

The entry ceiling is DIRECTION_ONLY for two compounding reasons:
1. The counter-factual inputs are INFERRED_STRUCTURAL (Tier 3) — no historical programme at these parameters was negotiated. The baseline (Troika path) is empirically anchored; the counter-factual branch is a structural inference.
2. The AEP-001 baseline itself has a DIRECTION_ONLY overall ceiling (see AEP-001 §6) — the branch comparison inherits this constraint.

**Calibration basis:** EURO-AREA family (Ilzetzki et al. 2013, CM-A), confidence_tier=2 — same as AEP-001. Counter-factual parameters INFERRED_STRUCTURAL from Blanchard–Leigh (2013) multiplier analysis retroactively applied to 2010 programme design.

**What a reader can rely on:**
- *For DIRECTION_ONLY:* The engine finds consistent, growing human development advantage under the counter-factual path from Step 2 onward. The direction claim is that a lighter fiscal programme produces better hd_composite outcomes within the model — not that this alternative was achievable or that the magnitude (+0.1657 at Step 6) is calibrated.

## 7. Known Limitations

- [x] **Counter-factual inputs are INFERRED_STRUCTURAL (Tier 3):** No historical programme at these parameters was negotiated for Greece. The lighter programme path was not on offer given creditor leverage in May 2010. The counter-factual is a structured analytical exercise bounded by DIRECTION_ONLY.
- [x] **fin_composite does not differentiate the two paths from Step 2:** Both branches converge to 0.0833. The fiscal multiplier model produces the same fin_composite outcome regardless of the front-loaded cut size once the initial impulse propagates. This is a model limitation — the gentler path should in principle produce a less negative fin_composite in subsequent steps, but the engine does not capture this path dependency.
- [x] **No failure modes detected:** Neither Backside_of_Power_Curve nor Coffin_Corner is detected at Steps 2–3, despite Greece 2012 being a canonical near-Coffin-Corner case. This is consistent with the known limitation in AEP-001 and reflects the absence of an endogenous failure mode detection module for the EURO-AREA scenario.
- [x] **Capital controls transmission gap (Issue #1532):** The Troika baseline applies capital controls at Step 6; the counter-factual does not. The Step 6 hd_composite divergence (+0.1657) captures the input-level difference but not the full bank-run / deposit-freeze transmission dynamics that the historical capital controls episode produced.
- [x] **hd_composite declining in both paths:** Both the counter-factual and baseline show monotonically declining hd_composite (0.8031 → 0.6619 for the counter-factual). The counter-factual slows the rate of decline but does not arrest it. This is consistent with a fiscal multiplier model that accumulates contractionary effects without a recovery or social resilience channel.
- [x] **G2C test advisory (INDISTINGUISHABLE) was a false signal (NM-101):** The G2C test returned INDISTINGUISHABLE because the baseline trajectory was empty (baseline never run before comparison). The correct verdict with a populated baseline is COUNTER_FACTUAL_BETTER. See NM-101 for process improvement.

## 8. Plain-Language Synthesis

The engine finds that the gradual adjustment counter-factual produces a meaningfully better human development trajectory than the documented Troika path from Step 2 (2011) onward — the divergence grows and stabilises at approximately 0.17 hd_composite units by Step 6. This is a directional claim only: the counter-factual inputs were not historically negotiated and the magnitude cannot be treated as calibrated. Both paths show declining human development throughout the 2010–2015 period; the counter-factual slows the rate of decline but does not reverse it. The finding is consistent with the Blanchard–Leigh (2013) retrospective analysis suggesting the programme's fiscal multiplier assumptions led to larger-than-expected output and welfare losses — but the engine is making this claim from an INFERRED_STRUCTURAL input design, not from validated historical data.
