## 2. Historical Context

Sri Lanka's 2022 crisis is the most analytically significant scenario in the WorldSim evidence base because it is the only documented real-world instance of all six aviation-derived failure modes firing simultaneously within an approximately 24-month window:

- **The Spin:** Accelerating debt spiral from pandemic revenue collapse, continued defence spending commitments, and the 2019 Sirisena→2020 Rajapaksa tax cuts that reduced government revenue by ~1.5% GDP
- **Coffin Corner:** Policy options narrowed simultaneously by depleting foreign reserves (from ~5 months import cover in 2019 to near-zero by April 2022), loss of Eurobond market access (August 2021, two months after the fertiliser ban), and the agricultural shock from the organic fertiliser mandate — each narrowing the exit corridor individually, all three closing it simultaneously
- **Hypoxia:** The organic fertiliser mandate (May 2021) was imposed under institutional delusion — projecting export benefits that contradicted available agronomy evidence; IMF engagement refused until reserves were in a critical position (April 2022)
- **Backside of Power Curve:** CBSL's currency peg defence depleted reserves faster than the trade deficit compressed — defending the 200 LKR/USD rate consumed ~USD 2.5bn in reserves in 2022 H1, accelerating the very crisis it was intended to prevent
- **Get-There-Itis:** 26 months of warning signals (rising EMBI spreads, falling reserves, food price inflation from mid-2021) passed before IMF engagement was accepted in April 2022
- **CB Cloud:** The Sri Lankan population experienced food shortages, medicine scarcity, and fuel queues (up to 13 hours daily) approximately 12–18 months before the government acknowledged the scale of the liquidity crisis

**Historical arc modelled (5 annual steps, 2019–2023):**
- **2019 (initial state):** Pre-crisis baseline. GDP +2.3%; reserves ~5.0 months; S&P B+ (credit score 29); EMBI ~90 bps
- **2020 (Step 1):** Pandemic shock — tourism revenue collapse (~10% GDP). GDP -3.6% (not modelled as explicit scheduled input; endogenous from initial state propagation)
- **2021 (Step 2):** Organic fertiliser ban (April 2021) — agricultural output collapsed ~40% in short-season crops; Eurobond market access lost (August 2021)
- **2022a (Step 3):** CBSL reserve defence (exchange rate intervention defending LKR peg); fuel subsidy continuation under political constraint
- **2022b (Step 4):** Debt moratorium (April 2022, USD 7.1bn suspended); formal sovereign default declaration (May 2022 — first default in Sri Lanka's post-independence history); President Rajapaksa fled, resigned July 2022
- **2023 (Step 5):** IMF Extended Fund Facility accepted (March 2023, USD 3bn, 48-month programme) following bilateral debt restructuring with China and India

The LKA scenario is the primary test case for the engine's ability to detect Coffin Corner dynamics, multi-failure-mode accumulation, and the asymmetric cost distribution between the political-financial elite and Q1 poverty-cohort households.

## 3. Question Posed

**Question:** Does the engine reproduce declining financial and human development composites across Sri Lanka's 2019–2022 crisis arc — consistent with the documented reserve depletion, GDP contraction, and human development deterioration — and does the default declaration at Step 4 produce a significant financial composite decline (consistent with the fin_composite collapse documented in AEP-007 for Argentina's default)?

**Scenario type rationale:** Type A historical replay. This is the SOUTH-SE-ASIAN family's most important test case for Coffin Corner detection. The fixture design intention was to test whether the engine can detect simultaneous multi-failure-mode accumulation across five steps.

## 5. Harness Output Summary

**Report reference:** `backend/tests/backtesting/reports/sri_lanka_2022_2023-2026-07-07-8750594.json`

**Overall verdict:** The engine produces NO declining composite signal across the crisis arc. Steps 1–3 are identically flat (fin=0.5927, hd=0.9000); Steps 4–5 show a counterintuitive *increase* in both composites at the default declaration and IMF SBA steps. This is the most complete fidelity failure in the AEP evidence base to date: the engine fails to detect any aspect of Sri Lanka's multi-failure-mode crisis.

**Per-step trajectory:**

| 1 | 2020 | 0.5927 | 0.9000 | [0.5038, 0.6816] | 1 | None | None |
| 2 | 2021 | 0.5927 | 0.9000 | [0.4149, 0.7705] | 1 | None | None |

**Trajectory narrative:**

**Steps 1–3 identically flat:** fin_composite holds at exactly 0.5927 and hd_composite at exactly 0.9000 across the first three steps. The engine produces zero response to: the pandemic shock (modelled as endogenous propagation from the 2019 initial state), the organic fertiliser ban (Step 2), and the CBSL reserve defence plus fuel subsidy continuation (Step 3). No composite differentiation is produced between the pre-crisis 2019 baseline and the acute Coffin Corner entry in 2022a.

**Steps 4–5 counterintuitive increase:** At Step 4 — the debt moratorium and sovereign default declaration — both composites *increase*: fin from 0.5927 to 0.6161 (+4.0%), hd from 0.9000 to 0.9134 (+1.5%). This is directionally inconsistent with the historical record and structurally inconsistent with the engine's own prior behaviour: in AEP-007 (Argentina 2002 default), the `default_declaration` input drove fin_composite to exactly 0.0000. The LKA default declaration, by contrast, produces a composite improvement.

The most likely explanation: the `debt_moratorium` input at Step 4 may be interpreted by the engine as debt service relief (a positive balance sheet event), with the subsequent `default_declaration` not overriding this signal. The net effect of Step 4's two inputs is counterintuitive — improvement rather than collapse.

Step 5 (IMF SBA acceptance) holds at the same elevated Step 4 level. This is consistent with GHA AEP-006 where the IMF programme produced initial stabilisation, but in LKA's case the composites were already at their incorrectly elevated Step 4 level.

**CI band expansion:** The CI band widens monotonically from Steps 1 to 3 ([0.5038, 0.6816] → [0.2815, 0.9039]) — nearly the full possible range by Step 3. The widening is appropriate for a T3-calibrated 5-step scenario, but the central estimate's failure to decline means the CI expansion provides only uncertainty information, not directional signal.

**PSP=1 throughout:** PSP remains at 1 across all five steps, including the default declaration step. No conditionality dynamics are modelled. This is consistent with PSP observations in other SOUTH-SE-ASIAN entries (PAK AEP-011).

**Calibration basis:** SOUTH-SE-ASIAN family — Batini, Callegari, and Melina (2012) + IMF APAC REO. All T3 (CM-C). No country-specific multiplier calibration for LKA. T2 data available for external balance (CBSL) and human development (DCS/WDI), but T3 multiplier is the binding constraint.

**Explanation of flat/improving trajectory:** Two structural explanations are most likely:

1. **Initial state effect:** The 2019 initial state is highly positive (GDP +2.3%, unemployment 4.8%, reserves 5.0 months, B+ credit rating, low EMBI spread). These positive attributes likely dominate the composite calculation throughout the early crisis. The SOUTH-SE-ASIAN T3 multiplier may not be large enough to pull the composites down against these positive initial conditions over five annual steps.

2. **Debt moratorium signal direction:** The `debt_moratorium` input may be modelled by the engine as providing debt service relief (reducing required payments, improving near-term balance sheet), with a net positive composite effect that overrides the distress signal. If so, Step 4's two inputs (moratorium + default_declaration) produce a net positive balance-sheet interpretation rather than a crisis signal.

**What a reader can rely on:**
- *For this entry:* No directional claim can be made. The engine does not reproduce the direction of Sri Lanka's 2022 crisis at any step. This entry is a documentation of engine limitation, not a capability demonstration. The most significant finding is the counterintuitive composite *increase* at the sovereign default step — a stronger failure than the flat/non-responsive patterns documented in other AEP entries.

## 7. Known Limitations

- [x] **Step 1 pandemic shock not captured:** The pandemic shock (2020 GDP -3.6%) is modelled as endogenous propagation from the 2019 initial state — no explicit scheduled input at Step 1. With a positive initial GDP (2.3%), the engine's endogenous dynamics from this starting point do not produce a deterioration signal. This is a fixture design limitation: the pandemic contraction should likely be an explicit scheduled input at Step 1 rather than relying on endogenous propagation from a pre-crisis baseline.

- [x] **Steps 1–3 complete insensitivity:** The organic fertiliser ban (regulatory_change), reserve defence (exchange_rate_intervention), and fuel subsidy continuation (spending_change) produce zero composite change from the initial state across three consecutive steps. This is more severe than the Steps 1–2 identical pattern in GHA AEP-006 (which at least showed decline from Step 3). For LKA, no scheduled input across three crisis steps produces any detectable composite response.

- [x] **Counterintuitive composite increase at default (Step 4):** The sovereign default declaration increases both fin_composite and hd_composite. This is structurally inconsistent with AEP-007's Argentina default result (fin→0.0000) and with any reasonable expectation of how a sovereign default affects financial composites. This is the most significant individual data point in the G3 evidence set — it indicates the engine's `debt_moratorium` + `default_declaration` combination does not produce a crisis signal for the SOUTH-SE-ASIAN entry-state conditions.

- [x] **All six failure modes not detected:** LKA 2022 is the canonical case for simultaneous six-failure-mode firing. No failure modes are detected at any step — not The Spin, Coffin Corner, Hypoxia, Backside of Power Curve, Get-There-Itis, or CB Cloud. This extends the failure mode non-detection finding from Issue #1797 (EURO-AREA rapid-onset crises) to the most extreme possible test case in the AEP portfolio. The failure mode detection system is not operating for this scenario.

- [x] **Remittance channel absent:** LKA remittances are ~8% GDP (DCS/CBSL). This is a structural positive stabilising channel that is absent from the engine's SOUTH-SE-ASIAN calibration. For the crisis arc, absence of the remittance channel means the Step 3 shock could be understated (disrupted remittances adding to the crisis) or overstated (continued remittances as a stabiliser). Direction is unclear; the channel is absent regardless.

- [x] **High initial hd_composite (0.9000) creates ceiling interpretation problem:** The 2019 initial state produces hd_composite=0.9000 — near the maximum. This makes it structurally difficult for the engine to detect deterioration: any shock must pull an already near-maximum value downward. The SOUTH-SE-ASIAN CM-C calibration, applied to a high-development starting point, may structurally limit the observable crisis signal range.

- [x] **PSP not populated:** PSP=1 throughout, including the default step and the IMF SBA step. Programme conditionality dynamics are absent from this entry. No SBA compliance signals are generated.

## 8. Plain-Language Synthesis

The engine does not find Sri Lanka's 2022 crisis. This is the shortest and most complete summary of what AEP-010 documents.

Across five steps covering what is arguably the most severe sovereign crisis in a middle-income country in the 2020s — including an organic fertiliser ban that collapsed agriculture, a sovereign default on USD 7.1 billion, and the forced exile of the President — the engine produces no declining composite signal. It produces the opposite: a small *increase* in both financial and human development composites when the default is declared.

This is a more significant finding than anything documented in G1 or G2. AEP-006 (GHA) showed declining composites but missed the CB Cloud distributional signal. AEP-007 (ARG) correctly collapsed fin_composite to zero at the default step. AEP-008 (ARG) was flat on fin_composite but correctly rising on hd. In all prior entries, the engine at minimum produced the correct directional signal for the most severe crisis event. In AEP-010, the engine produces the wrong direction at the most severe event (Step 4: default) and no signal at all for the preceding three crisis steps.

For a Sri Lankan finance ministry analyst, this entry demonstrates what WorldSim currently cannot do: it cannot model the Coffin Corner dynamics that define Sri Lanka's specific crisis type. The six-failure-mode simultaneous firing, the policy option narrowing, the asymmetric cost distribution to Q1 households — none of these register. The analytical value of this entry is in its honest disclosure of the engine's current scope limitation for this class of scenario.
