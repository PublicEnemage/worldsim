## 2. Historical Context

The Kirchner recovery period (2003–2007) is analytically distinct from the crisis arc documented in AEP-007. Where AEP-007 covers the default and collapse (2001–2003), this entry covers what followed: a sustained V-shaped recovery unprecedented in scale among post-default emerging markets of the period.

Néstor Kirchner took office in May 2003 after Eduardo Duhalde's interim stabilisation. The recovery was anchored by several structural factors operating simultaneously: (1) fiscal normalisation — the 2002 emergency contraction rebounded as government services resumed and social programs expanded; (2) peso undervaluation (the post-devaluation exchange rate suppressed import competition and boosted export competitiveness); (3) a soybean commodity boom — international soybean prices rose sharply from 2003, and Argentina's pampas-based agriculture sector benefited substantially, generating export tax revenues that funded fiscal expansion without IMF conditionality; (4) utility tariff suppression — domestic utility tariffs were held below market rates, subsidising household and industrial consumption during the recovery.

GDP grew 8.8% in 2003, 9.0% in 2004, 8.9% in 2005, and 8.0% in 2006. Unemployment fell from a 21.5% peak in 2002 to 8.7% by 2006. Poverty headcount declined from approximately 57% at the 2002 trough to approximately 26% by 2006 (INDEC EPHC). This is a documented, large-scale human development improvement driven by heterodox fiscal and monetary policy in the aftermath of sovereign default — a scenario with high analytical relevance for the WorldSim mission.

**CM-D construction:** No pre-existing fixture file existed for the 2003–2007 period. This scenario was constructed from the CM-D calibration decision (`docs/calibration/m19-cm-d-arg-kirchner-calibration-decision.md`), which established Step 3 of the AEP-007 fixture as +0.030 GDP net fiscal impulse proxy. The standalone Kirchner scenario uses CM-D's Step 3 value as Step 1, and adds subsequent steps from MECON Budget Execution 2003 and IMF WEO April 2004–2008. All inputs are T3 (INFERRED_STRUCTURAL).

**Trajectory narrative:**

**hd_composite:** Rises from 0.5143 at Step 1 to 0.5634 at Step 3, then stabilises. The directional improvement across Steps 1–3 is consistent with Argentina's documented 2003–2005 human development recovery: declining unemployment, falling poverty headcount, rising household consumption. The Step 4 flat value (same as Step 3) suggests the engine has reached a plateau under the moderating fiscal impulse at Step 4 — the smaller +1.0% GDP spending_change does not add further hd_composite momentum.

**fin_composite:** Exactly 0.7292 across all four steps. The fin_composite shows no change whatsoever across the entire four-step recovery arc — neither the fiscal normalisation nor the subsequent steps produce any fin_composite signal. This is the most severe instance of fin_composite path insensitivity (Issue #1796) observed across G1 and G2 entries: four steps of positive fiscal impulses, and the fin_composite remains identically 0.7292. The engine cannot differentiate between a country at 8.8% growth recovering from default and a country at the same fiscal-impulse level in a different structural context.

**CI band expansion:** The CI band expands dramatically from Step 1 ([0.6198, 0.8386]) to Step 3–4 ([0.3464, 1.000]) — the upper bound hits 1.0 (the ceiling), and the lower bound declines to 0.35. By Steps 3–4, the confidence interval is essentially uninformative: the full range of possible outcomes (0.35 to 1.0) is represented.

PSP is 1 throughout — no active programme conditionality dynamics.

## 7. Known Limitations

- [x] **Constructed scenario — no pre-existing fixture:** The AEP-008 scenario was constructed in-session from CM-D calibration decision parameters. No validated historical fixture exists for ARG 2003–2007. All inputs are T3 (INFERRED_STRUCTURAL). This is the most significant limitation of this entry.
- [x] **fin_composite path insensitivity (#1796):** fin_composite is identically 0.7292 across all four steps. The engine cannot differentiate Argentina's 2003–2006 recovery path at the financial composite level. This is the maximum expression of the path insensitivity gap documented in G1: not only does the engine not differentiate between two different policy paths (as in G1), it cannot produce any fin_composite movement across four years of fiscal expansion. The hd_composite signal is the only available indicator.
- [x] **CI band hitting ceiling at Steps 3–4:** Upper CI bound reaches 1.0 by Step 3. This is a calibration artefact — T3 uncertainty accumulation over 4 steps with positive fiscal impulses pushes the upper bound to the model ceiling. Steps 3–4 CI values are not interpretable for magnitude assessment.
- [x] **Step 4 hd recovery not captured:** Historical data shows continued poverty and unemployment improvement in 2006. The engine produces the same hd_composite at Step 4 as Step 3 (0.5634), suggesting the smaller fiscal impulse (+1.0% GDP at Step 4) does not produce additional momentum. Momentum effects from multi-year recovery trajectories are not captured.
- [x] **CM-D authority limited to Step 1:** The CM-D calibration decision was specifically for Step 3 of the AEP-007 fixture (+0.030 GDP spending_change). The CM-D value is reused as Step 1 of this constructed scenario, but the subsequent steps (Step 2–4 here) are constructed from IMF WEO data without formal CM calibration. Steps 2–4 inputs are INFERRED_STRUCTURAL at a lower confidence level than Step 1.
- [x] **Soybean boom and exchange rate dynamics not modelled:** A major driver of Argentina's 2003–2007 recovery was the peso undervaluation (export competitiveness) and soybean commodity boom (export tax revenue). Neither channel is explicitly modelled in the constructed scenario inputs. The fiscal impulse from soybean taxes is approximated through the spending_change inputs, but the commodity price effect is not separated.

## 8. Plain-Language Synthesis

The engine finds that Argentina's 2003–2007 Kirchner recovery, when modelled from CM-D calibration decision inputs, produces a rising hd_composite across Steps 1–3 — directionally consistent with the documented decline in unemployment and poverty headcount during this period. This is the correct signal: fiscal normalisation after a default does improve human development outcomes in the model.

The fin_composite is entirely flat (0.7292 across all steps) — the engine cannot detect or reproduce financial recovery dynamics for Argentina's debt restructuring and reserve rebuilding period. This is the clearest demonstration to date of the fin_composite path insensitivity gap (Issue #1796): not a subtle limitation but a complete absence of fin_composite response across four years of documented recovery.

The CI band becomes uninformative by Step 3–4 (upper bound at ceiling). The only reliable directional signal from this entry is hd_composite improvement across the first three steps. For a finance ministry analyst, this provides the minimum useful signal: the engine finds that heterodox fiscal recovery from a sovereign default eventually produces human development improvement — but it cannot tell you at what rate, or whether the financial recovery is on track.
