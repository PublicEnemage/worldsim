## 2. Historical Context

In May 2010, the Greek government faced imminent sovereign default. Accumulated fiscal deficits — exacerbated by pre-crisis accounting irregularities that had understated the true deficit scale — triggered a market confidence collapse that pushed 10-year Greek-German spreads to 850 basis points by January 2010, widening to over 1,500 bps by year-end and approaching 2,000 bps at the 2012 near-default. The eurozone's first major member-state crisis forced an unprecedented choice: accept IMF and EU conditionality or default without a framework for eurozone exit.

Greece accepted a €110bn ESM/IMF programme in May 2010 — equivalent to 48% of GDP — with conditionality requiring approximately 8% of GDP in immediate spending cuts (Step 1), followed by successive austerity packages in 2011 and 2012. A third memorandum in 2012 included private sector involvement (PSI), effectively constituting a partial default on privately held debt. Primary surplus conditionality was eventually met in 2013 (+1.5% GDP).

The human cost was severe. GDP contracted by approximately 25% cumulatively over 2010–2016 — the deepest peacetime depression in modern European history. Unemployment rose from 12.7% in early 2010 to 27.5% at its 2013 peak. Youth unemployment exceeded 60%. Poverty rates doubled. Net emigration of approximately 500,000 working-age Greeks, primarily highly educated, accelerated from 2012 onward. Capital controls were imposed on 26 June 2015 during the Tsipras government's creditor standoff — a bank-run dynamic that threatened complete financial system collapse. The known historical outcome is one of fiscal consolidation achieved at extraordinary human cost, with contraction far exceeding programme projections.

## 3. Question Posed

**Question:** Does the engine correctly reproduce the direction of GDP contraction across the Troika adjustment programme period (2010–2015), consistent with the documented historical outcome of five consecutive years of negative growth interrupted only by a brief partial recovery in 2014?

**Scenario type rationale:** This is a Type A replay — the scheduled inputs replicate the documented historical programme conditionality sequence, and the harness output is evaluated against the known outturn data. No counter-factual branch is introduced.

## 7. Known Limitations

- [x] **No endogenous recovery module (Issue #221):** GDP growth at step 5 (2014 historical: +0.7%) is not reproduced. The engine accumulates contraction without a mean-reversion or growth recovery channel. The CALIBRATED_CI claim on fiscal trajectory applies to steps 1–4 and step 6 only. This is the primary fidelity gap for this entry.
- [x] **No endogenous unemployment module (Issue #87):** The engine does not update `unemployment_rate` endogenously. The unemployment direction in the austerity phase (step 1→2 rising) is DEFERRED PASS — the direction matches but is not enforced in CI. The declining phase (steps 4–6) is DEFERRED FAIL — the engine does not reproduce the peak and subsequent decline. Magnitude claims are valid for the rising phase only.
- [x] **No endogenous health expenditure module (Issue #87):** `health_expenditure_pct_gdp` is held flat in the engine. The historical decline from 9.5% (2010) to 8.7% (2012) is documented but not reproduced. This is a blindspot, not an incorrect output.
- [x] **No investment climate modules (Issue #92):** Sovereign risk premium, FDI stock, credit rating score, and portfolio flow velocity are not updated endogenously. These attributes are seeded from initial state data but remain flat throughout the run. All four represent documented blindspots — the engine cannot reproduce the credit cascade, capital flight, or rating downgrade sequence.
- [x] **Capital controls transmission gap (Issue #1532):** The Step 6 capital controls input fires the EmergencyPolicyInput but capital control transmission channels (deposit outflows, import compression, credit tightening) are not fully modelled. The GDP direction verdict at step 6 is PASS but the mechanism of transmission is incomplete.
- [x] **Ecological composite is CO2 proximity only:** The land-use boundary (Richardson 2023) post-dates the scenario period. Ecological cost distribution within Greece (regional energy mix, agricultural sector) is not modelled.
- [x] **Reserve coverage is an initial state condition:** The CRITICAL MDA alert fires because the initial state seeds below the 2.5-month floor. The engine does not model reserve accumulation or depletion dynamics — the breach persists as a constant rather than a trajectory outcome. The MDA alert is an honest representation of the initial liquidity position, not a trajectory finding.

## 8. Plain-Language Synthesis

The engine consistently reproduces downward GDP direction across the Troika adjustment programme — five of six annual steps show negative growth, matching the documented historical pattern of a protracted contraction. Reserve coverage begins below the critical MDA floor and remains there throughout, reflecting Greece's entry into the programme in a structurally weak liquidity position. The engine does not reproduce the brief 2014 growth recovery or the investment climate deterioration (sovereign spread widening, capital flight, credit downgrades) — these are documented blindspots rather than incorrect predictions, and they limit the entry to DIRECTION_ONLY overall. The fiscal trajectory dimension, however, achieves CALIBRATED_CI bounds via the EURO-AREA multiplier calibration: the contraction direction at steps 1–4 and 6 is not just asserted, it is bounded by the Ilzetzki et al. posterior.
