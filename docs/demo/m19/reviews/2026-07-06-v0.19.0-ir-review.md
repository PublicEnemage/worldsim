## Part 1 — Per-Frame Analysis

### Frame A — "Constraint Found" (ZMB Demo 8 Act1, Mode 3 Active Control, Step 4/8)

**What the frame is presenting:** The Mode 3 constraint-floor search has completed. The fiscal multiplier floor of ≥0.83 is displayed in the CONSTRAINT SEARCH panel. Poverty headcount is CLEAR at 0.465 against a floor of 0.400. Zone 1A shows the active branch above the floor threshold.

**What is visible:**

The CONSTRAINT SEARCH result panel reads: "Safe boundary found: fiscal multiplier ≥ 0.83 · ±0.01 precision · 9 evaluations · [0.1, 3.0] searched · This is the binary search precision, not a statistical confidence interval." This is the demo's primary deliverable and is clearly readable. The "binary search precision, not a statistical confidence interval" disclosure is well-placed and technically honest.

Zone 1B COHORT IMPACT shows a CLEAR badge: "bottom quintile informal workers poverty headcount / 0.465 / floor 0.400." This is legible and supports the constraint-found narrative.

Zone 1D: Financial 0.51 (+0.04), Human Development 0.46 (+0.03), Ecological "Not modelled," Governance 0.49 (+0.00). Human Development has equal colour and size treatment to Financial — this satisfies the equal-weight founding principle.

**IR-M19-001 (HIGH):** The Zone 1A dropdown selector reads "Gdp Usd Millions (USD_millions_cur..." The chart Y-axis runs from approximately 0.35 to 0.55 — values that cannot represent GDP in USD millions, which would be in the thousands or tens of thousands range. The floor annotation at 0.40 corresponds to the poverty headcount floor specified in the CONSTRAINT SEARCH panel ("Poverty headcount floor: ≥0.400"), not to any GDP threshold. A stakeholder reading Zone 1A will see a label saying "GDP in USD Millions" on a chart with normalized values (0.35–0.55) and a floor line at 0.40 — these three elements do not compose a coherent reading. The opening frame of the constraint-found argument should leave no ambiguity about what the chart is measuring.

**IR-M19-008 (LOW):** Zone 1C reads "All thresholds breached — see alerts" in red text. Zone 1B simultaneously shows a CLEAR badge on the poverty headcount cohort row and only one active alert (Reserve Coverage at 12.0% above floor). The "all thresholds breached" language suggests comprehensive failure while the dominant cohort status says CLEAR. The apparent contradiction is unexplained. A stakeholder watching the presenter demonstrate a "safe boundary found" result while the screen displays "all thresholds breached" will notice the inconsistency.

Human Cost Ledger: Three of five entries ("Bottom Quintile...", "Income Share...", "Access to Health...") show "—". Poverty 54.0% [T3·synthetic] and Unemployment 18.1% [T3·synthetic] are present. The equal-weight principle requires human cost indicators to be visible alongside financial indicators; three blank fields in the human cost section weakens that parity at the frame level.

**Verdict: Mixed.** The constraint-found result and cohort impact CLEAR badge are readable and support the primary argument. IR-M19-001 (GDP label mismatch) and IR-M19-008 (zone contradiction) both require resolution or explicit presenter briefing.

This is presumably a placeholder reserved for Mode 3 that is appropriately empty in Replay mode. But the visible text reads to a stakeholder as an incomplete or placeholder UI — particularly because Frames A–C showed a fully populated right panel with policy inputs and constraint search controls. **IR-M19-006 (MEDIUM):** POLITICAL RISK section shows "Option A: —", "Option B: —", "Option C: —" — all three blank. **IR-M19-002 (HIGH):** The methodology panel contains multiple items that are internal developer nomenclature, visible on screen during a stakeholder demonstration:

- "G3 #1537" — a sprint group issue identifier. A finance ministry official will ask: "What is G3 #1537?" This erodes the professional register of the tool.
- "BandingEngine CalibrationStore" — an internal component name. Audible if read aloud; visible on screen.
- "MAGNITUDE_MATCH gate" — an internal system flag. The concept (a quality gate that unlocks empirical calibration) is legitimate to communicate; the internal name is not the right language for this audience.
- "'ZMB:CHT:1-*'" — an internal entity ID pattern. A finance ministry analyst seeing "ZMB:CHT:1-*" will not know if this refers to Zambia cohort data, a configuration key, or a code artifact. It reads as raw system internals leaking into a stakeholder interface.
- "SEN calibration entry CAL-001 provisional" — a Senegal calibration is referenced as provisional in a Zambia methodology panel. The cross-entity provisional reference may prompt a question about whether Zambia's own calibration is also provisional.

This finding is categorically different from the M18 equivalent (IR-M18-005, LOW). The M18 methodology panel said "T3 placeholder pending ADR-007 full CI band integration" — developer-adjacent language but communicating a substantive limitation. The M19 panel has moved materially further into internal system nomenclature. A moderately technically literate participant in the room — not an expert, just an analyst who reads carefully — will notice "G3 #1537" and "MAGNITUDE_MATCH gate" and draw conclusions about the tool's maturity that the rest of the demo does not support.

**Verdict: Requires targeted editing before stakeholder session.** The methodology provenance (population source, CI width rationale, tier basis, forward calibration path) is the right content for this panel and is epistemically honest. The presentation layer needs to translate internal component names into substantive descriptions.

## Part 2 — Cross-Frame Findings

### Human Cost Ledger Static Values (IR-M19-005, MEDIUM)

Across all five frames, the Human Cost Ledger shows Poverty 54.0% and Unemployment 18.1% as fixed values. In Frames A–C, the fiscal multiplier is demonstrated to produce a CLEAR result on the bottom quintile poverty headcount cohort. The Zone 1D Human Development composite changes (0.46 → 0.43 between steps 4 and 8). Yet the headline poverty percentage in the Human Cost Ledger never moves.

A development economist in the room will notice this. The COHORT IMPACT in Zone 1B shows the poverty headcount ratio at 0.445–0.465 (44.5–46.5%), which is broadly consistent with a 54% poverty figure (the COHORT IMPACT may be measuring a specific cohort while the 54% is the aggregate). But to an audience that just watched a Mode 3 constraint search demonstrate a safe fiscal multiplier path, seeing "Poverty 54.0%" unchanged across every step of the run — including after the safe boundary is applied and verified — raises a legitimate question: "Did the policy actually change anything for the poor?"

The founding principle states that human cost indicators must have equal visual weight to financial indicators. In Frames A–C, the financial composite changes (+0.04 at step 4, returns to 0.00 at step 8) and is prominently displayed in the header bar. The human cost headline numbers never change. Visual parity is present at the composite score level (Zone 1D) but absent at the headline ledger level.

### Mode and Scenario Transition (Cross-frame narrative gap, no separate finding ID)

Frames A–C are ZMB Demo 8 Act1, Mode 3 Active Control. Frames D–E are ZMB Demo 8 OptionC, Replay mode. The transition involves both a mode change and a scenario change with no visual bridge. Within the frame sequence, there is no element that marks the transition or explains it. This is a walkthrough/presenter-design issue rather than a UI defect, and it is noted here rather than as a separate finding because it cannot be addressed by changes to the screenshots themselves.

## Part 3 — North Star Assessment

**Scenario:** A Zambian Finance Ministry team of three economists is meeting with an IMF technical mission. The IMF has presented their preferred fiscal adjustment programme (Option A). The ministry team has prepared a counter-proposal (Option C) with a smaller fiscal consolidation. The IMF team's position is that poverty outcomes under Option C are indistinguishable from Option A within the model's uncertainty range. The ministry team has just run Demo 8.

**Does Demo 8 change what the ministry team can argue at the table?**

**Act 1 (constraint search) — yes, with conditions:** The ministry team can say: "We ran a binary search over the fiscal multiplier range 0.1 to 3.0. The minimum multiplier that keeps the bottom quintile informal workers poverty headcount above the 0.40 recovery floor is 0.83 — reached in 9 evaluations. The programme's multiplier assumption is 0.85. The margin above the floor is 12.5%, not structural. At a multiplier of 0.82, the threshold is crossed." Frame A provides the on-screen evidence for this argument provided the presenter addresses IR-M19-001 (the GDP label mismatch undermines the chart's readability). Frame C closes the argument with the CLEAR badge at step 8.

**Act 2 (distributional comparison) — yes:** The ministry team can say: "Under your preferred programme (Option A), our simulation places 342,727 more persons below the poverty threshold compared to Option C at the terminal step. The declared interval is 298,000 to 398,000. The direction is stable across the full uncertainty range — the sign does not flip under any configuration in that interval. Under T3 demographic weights from ECOWAS regional comparables, Option C produces a superior distributional outcome. We would like to see your distributional model for the same comparison." That question — "show us your distributional model" — changes the negotiating structure. Frame D delivers this argument cleanly.

**Act 2 (methodology panel) — conditional:** If asked to defend the CI bounds, the ministry team can open the methodology panel (Frame E) and show the computation chain. However, IR-M19-002 (internal nomenclature) means that a technically literate IMF economist reading "G3 #1537" and "MAGNITUDE_MATCH gate" in the live panel will question whether they are looking at a production-quality tool or a development artefact. This risks undermining the credibility of the prior argument precisely when the team is under challenge.

**North Star verdict:** The primary capability — a software tool that finds the safe multiplier boundary (Act 1) and quantifies the distributional differential with an interval and direction-stability declaration (Act 2) — is demonstrably present in these five frames. The finance ministry team can argue the positions above. Two issues create risk: the GDP label mismatch weakens the opening frame, and the internal nomenclature in the methodology panel creates credibility exposure when the tool's methodology is challenged. Neither destroys the argument; both create unnecessary friction at the moments the audience is most attentive.

## Overall Verdict

**CONDITIONAL PASS**

The core analytical capabilities — Mode 3 constraint-floor search, COHORT IMPACT CLEAR badge, three-scenario distributional comparison with human count differential and declared uncertainty interval — are present and readable in the five frames. The demo's primary argument can be made and defended. No finding completely prevents the demo from proceeding.

Two conditions must be satisfied before the live stakeholder session:

### Condition 1 (IR-M19-001 — HIGH): Zone 1A dropdown metric in Frame A

Frame A must be recaptured with the dropdown showing the poverty headcount ratio (as in Frame B) rather than "Gdp Usd Millions (USD_millions_cur...". A chart with a floor at 0.40 corresponding to a poverty headcount constraint, labelled as GDP in USD millions, is incoherent to a stakeholder audience. If recapture is not possible, the presenter must be explicitly briefed that Zone 1A in Frame A is NOT to be read as a GDP chart — with specific language prepared for any question about the chart's units.

### Condition 2 (IR-M19-002 — HIGH): Internal nomenclature in Frame E methodology panel

The following strings must be replaced with stakeholder-legible language before the live session, or the methodology panel must not be shown in the live demo:

- "G3 #1537" → remove entirely or replace with "current sprint"
- "BandingEngine CalibrationStore" → "calibration engine"
- "MAGNITUDE_MATCH gate" → "empirical quality gate"
- "entities matching 'ZMB:CHT:1-*'" → "Zambia bottom quintile cohort data"
- "SEN calibration entry CAL-001 provisional" → "Senegal reference calibration (provisional)"

The substantive content of the methodology panel — population source, CI width rationale, tier basis, forward calibration path — is appropriate for a technical audience and should be retained.

### Additional pre-session actions (should complete, do not block the demo)

- **IR-M19-003**: Update dropdown label "(—, —)" notation or brief presenter to not draw attention to it
- **IR-M19-004**: Brief presenter that the empty right panel in Frames D/E is Mode 3 reserved space, not a UI bug; a brief spoken acknowledgement during the demo will prevent a credibility question
- **IR-M19-005**: Brief presenter to address the static Human Cost Ledger values during walkthrough — the distinction between the aggregate poverty percentage and the cohort-specific poverty headcount ratio in COHORT IMPACT requires one explanatory sentence
- **IR-M19-006**: If political risk data for the three options is available, populate it before the live session; if not, brief presenter to acknowledge the blank when transitioning to Frame D
- **IR-M19-007**: Confirm that Frame B presenter narration explicitly calls out the PSP driver sequence location; do not assume the audience will find it independently
