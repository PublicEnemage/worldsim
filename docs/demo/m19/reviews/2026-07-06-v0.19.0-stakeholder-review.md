## Demo Scenario Presented

### Act 1 — Zambia, Mode 3 Constraint-Floor Search

- **Country:** Zambia (ZMB)
- **Context:** IMF Extended Credit Facility — debt restructuring, fiscal programme design
- **Mode:** Mode 3 Active Control — constraint-floor search, Form 3
- **Constraint specified:** poverty headcount ≥ 0.40 across all eight programme steps
- **Search method:** Binary search, 8 iterations, convergence at fiscal multiplier ≥ 0.83 (±0.01)
- **Branch applied at:** FOUND state, step 4 (Zone 1B CLEAR: 0.465 / floor 0.400)
- **Programme window:** 8 steps
- **PSP driver arc:** Fiscal sustainability dominant steps 1–5; Social stability transitions to dominant at step 6; Social stability dominant steps 6–8 (Zone 1D sequential bold marker)
- **Act 1 finding:** Safe boundary found — fiscal multiplier ≥ 0.83 avoids poverty headcount floor crossing across all eight steps. Constraint search required zero manual slider iterations.
- **Primary thesis:** Given the constraint "bottom quintile informal workers poverty headcount does not fall below 0.40 at any programme step," what is the minimum fiscal multiplier that satisfies this constraint? The instrument answers. The analyst does not search manually.

### Act 2 — Zambia, Three-Scenario Comparison

- **Country:** Zambia (ZMB)
- **Context:** Debt restructuring — three competing programme proposals (same as Demo 7)
- **Scenarios:** Option A (EFF Front-Loaded), Option B (EFF Gradual), Option C (Homegrown Programme)
- **Reference scenario:** Option C (Ministry position)
- **Primary finding:** "Option A vs. Option C: +342,727 persons below poverty threshold · declared interval 298,000–398,000 · posterior CI active (structural prior ±13–16%, T3 baseline) · Direction stable"
- **Act 2 claim:** The posterior calibration layer is active (ADR-007 §8.2–8.5); the Senegal reference calibration provides provisional direction-only grounding; ZMB-specific calibration pending.

**Finance minister scenario:**

Aicha Mbaye — Finance Minister, Zambia equivalent. IMF Extended Credit Facility restructuring session. The ministry team has run WorldSim Mode 3 constraint-floor search before the meeting. The instrument was given the constraint: "bottom quintile informal workers poverty headcount must not fall below 0.40 at any programme step." The instrument returned: fiscal multiplier ≥ 0.83 (boundary found, step 4, 8 binary search iterations, ±0.01 precision). Zone 1B reads CLEAR at 0.465/0.400 at step 4 and CLEAR at 0.445/0.400 at programme completion. The distributional comparison shows Option C (Ministry counter-proposal) produces 342,727 fewer people below the poverty threshold than Option A (IMF front-loaded proposal), declared interval 298,000–398,000, direction stable.

**Capability confirmed live in this session:**

The ministry's analyst specified the poverty headcount floor constraint in Mode 3 Form 3. The instrument ran 8 binary search iterations and returned: fiscal multiplier ≥ 0.83. The analyst did not move the slider. The analyst did not test values one by one. The instrument tested the values and returned the boundary.

At the table: the ministry team's laptop shows Frame C — programme complete, CLEAR, "Safe boundary found: fiscal multiplier ≥ 0.83 ±0.01." The distributional comparison on Frame D shows the consequence of the boundary: 342,727 additional people in the IMF proposal vs. the Ministry counter-proposal.

**The specific argument at the table:**

"We asked the instrument: what is the minimum fiscal multiplier that keeps the poverty headcount above the 0.40 recovery floor across the entire programme window? The answer is 0.83.

"Your proposed programme embeds a multiplier assumption above 0.83. Under that assumption, the bottom quintile poverty headcount crosses the 0.40 recovery floor. The instrument did not find this by testing one value — it specified a constraint and found the boundary. The boundary is 0.83.

"Under your proposed programme's multiplier assumption, 342,727 additional people fall below the poverty threshold compared to the Ministry counter-proposal. The declared interval is 298,000 to 398,000. The direction is stable across the full uncertainty range.

"We are not saying your proposed multiplier assumption is wrong. We are saying the threshold crossing is a structural consequence of the multiplier assumption you have embedded — not of the adjustment itself. The constraint-floor boundary is 0.83. Your assumption is above 0.83. What does your distributional model show at 0.83?"

**Does this change what the ministry team can argue at the table?**

Yes — and it changes *what kind* of argument the ministry team can make, in a way that advances beyond Demo 7.

In Demo 7, the argument was: "We tested 0.85 and the threshold was not crossed." That is a tested-value argument. The IMF team's response to a tested-value argument is: "Your test was at 0.85. Our assumption is different. Why did you choose 0.85?"

In Demo 8, the argument is: "We specified the constraint. The instrument found the boundary. The boundary is 0.83." This is a constraint-boundary argument. The IMF team's response to a constraint-boundary argument is: "Your instrument says 0.83. Show us the methodology." The ministry team opens Zone 3. The methodology is on screen.

The difference is not rhetorical — it is analytical. A tested-value argument can be deflected by questioning the choice of test value. A constraint-boundary argument cannot be deflected by questioning the choice — the boundary is derived from the constraint, which the IMF team provided in the form of the programme design. The ministry team did not choose 0.83. The constraint chose 0.83.

**North star test verdict: PASS**

No conditions on the core capability. The constraint-floor boundary is on screen. The distributional consequence is on screen. The ministry team can state the argument without economist mediation.

One structural limitation remains on record — DEMO-217/DEMO-233: the Act1→OptionC navigation requires choreography that is not embedded in the instrument architecture. The ministry team must pre-load both scenarios and know the navigation path. This is the known gap for M20. It does not affect the north star verdict for Demo 8 because the demonstration showed that a choreographed team can assemble both findings in under 45 seconds — which is within a reasonable session window with preparation.
