### UX Design Thinking Agent

**Lens:** Mode-specific cognitive task alignment, instrument legibility, NARRATION-RULING-1
conformance.

**Mode 1 (Replay) primary cognitive tasks:** Trajectory reconstruction AND historical pattern
recognition. The demo must support both within its four steps.

**Trajectory reconstruction:** The four-step Argentina arc (programme entry → default →
recovery → consolidation) is supported by Zone 1A across all frames. The step annotations
on the x-axis provide the temporal anchoring required for trajectory reconstruction. Frame E's
full arc view is the strongest expression of this task.

**Historical pattern recognition:** The governance-financial divergence (institutional
recovery lags financial recovery) is the pattern being claimed. Frame C (the thesis frame)
should make this pattern visible without narration. The divergence is visible in Zone 1A
at step 3, but requires the presenter to name which curve is which — the legend is too small
to allow independent reading at presentation scale.

**NARRATION-RULING-1 assessment (three-layer structure):**
The walkthrough has been updated per PR #654. Review is limited to what can be assessed
from the screenshots combined with the walkthrough text.

The screenshot brief §Key Narration Notes #2 explicitly requires: "The dashed curve
convention needs one sentence of explanation." This requirement is not reflected in the
current walkthrough. At Step 2 of the walkthrough (where Zone 1 instruments are first
described), there is no sentence explaining what the dashed vs solid curves mean. A
non-specialist audience seeing two dashed and two solid curves will not understand the
methodological distinction without being told.

**Findings:**

```
[DEMO-013]: Dashed curve convention not narrated — screenshot brief requirement unimplemented
Severity: MEDIUM
Screenshot ref: frame-a (dashed curves visible in Zone 1A legend)
What is shown: Zone 1A shows two dashed curves (Financial, Human Development — Path A /
  normalized absolute strategy) and two solid curves (Ecological, Governance — boundary
  proximity and percentile strategies). The dashed vs solid distinction is methodologically
  significant: dashed curves indicate a scoring strategy that requires cautious interpretation
  for single-entity scenarios.
What should be shown: The walkthrough should include one sentence orienting the audience to
  the convention: "Two curves are dashed — Financial and Human Development — because they use
  a normalized scoring strategy designed for multi-country comparison. We acknowledge this
  openly in the methodology note." The screenshot brief explicitly required this narration
  (§Key Narration Notes #2) but it was not implemented in the walkthrough.
Recommendation: Add one sentence to walkthrough Step 2 (Zone 1 orientation) explaining
  the dashed/solid distinction. This closes the gap between the screenshot brief spec and
  the walkthrough implementation.
```

### Chief Methodologist (DIC)

**Lens:** No False Precision — uncertainty signals, bands, tiers correctly surfaced.

**Guiding principle:** "Outputs are distributions, not point estimates. Uncertainty is
quantified and displayed, not hidden. The model's blindspots are documented and visible."

**ADR-007 synthetic data framework:** Not implemented in M10. No pessimistic/realistic/
optimistic bands are expected. The demo correctly presents single-value outputs for the
Argentina fixture. No false precision concern from absent bands — the framework simply isn't
deployed yet.

**PMM as precision signal:** PMM of approximately 0.29 at step 1 (governance headroom of
DQS 0.71 vs. floor 0.70) is a very tight margin — roughly 1.4% of the threshold value.
Presenting this as a two-decimal value without confidence interval implies precision that
may not be warranted given that the governance seed data (V-Dem LDI ARG 2000 = 0.71) is
itself a point estimate from a survey-based index. The No False Precision principle applies
to PMM computation inputs.

**Governance composite at step 3 (0.665):** The walkthrough cites this value precisely.
The WGI Rule of Law percentile (33.2) and V-Dem LDI (0.71) inputs that produce this
composite are Tier 1–2 data. The precision of 0.665 is defensible given the sources.

**Zone 1A dashed curve confidence disclosure:** The dashed line style and "(single-country
index)" label are the primary No False Precision signals for the Financial and HD composites.
Both are present. The audience cannot read them at projected-display scale without narration
(see DEMO-012, DEMO-013).

**Findings:**

```
[DEMO-016]: PMM precision at tight governance margin may overstate certainty
Severity: LOW
Screenshot ref: frame-a (Zone 1C)
What is shown: PMM of ~0.29 at step 1. This reflects governance headroom of DQS 0.71 vs.
  floor 0.70 — a margin of 0.01 on an index derived from survey-based WGI/V-Dem point
  estimates that themselves carry uncertainty ranges not currently propagated into PMM.
What should be shown: Either: (a) a Zone 1C tooltip or hover note stating "margin calculated
  from point estimates — confidence interval not yet computed," consistent with the "No False
  Precision" principle; or (b) the walkthrough narration explicitly acknowledging the
  margin's dependence on point-estimate inputs at the step where PMM is first discussed.
Recommendation: Add a narration note to the walkthrough at the PMM first-mention step:
  "The margin you see is calculated from point estimates — at this stage, treat it as
  directional, not definitive." This is a low-cost disclosure consistent with CLAUDE.md
  §No False Precision.
```

### Development Economist (DIC)

**Lens:** Human cost ledger — is human impact given equal visual weight to financial
indicators?

**CLAUDE.md principle:** "The human cost ledger is never a footnote. It is a primary output
with equal visual weight to financial indicators."

**Zone 1A — Human Development curve:** The HD composite curve is present in Zone 1A
alongside Financial, Ecological, and Governance. All four curves carry equal visual weight
(same line weight, same axis scale). This is correct — the HD curve is not a footnote.

**Zone 1D — Human Development row:** The four-framework scorecard in Zone 1D presents
Financial, Human Development, Ecological, and Governance rows with equal visual treatment.
In frame A, the HD composite score appears to be approximately 0.34 — significantly below
Financial (~0.87) and Governance (~0.71). This HD score of 0.34 is, arguably, the most
important number in the entire demo: it represents the persistent human cost of Argentina's
structural poverty at programme entry, before the default even occurs.

**The walkthrough narration at step 4:** Frame E shows the recovery state. The walkthrough
Step 7 narration ("The GDP numbers — which you can see in the indicator panel") directs
attention to the financial recovery (+9% GDP). The HD composite at step 4 — whether it
remains at 0.34 or has shifted — is not explicitly narrated. Argentina's Kirchner recovery
was a GDP recovery, not a human development recovery in the short term. The HD score's
trajectory through the four steps is the human cost story — and it is not narrated.

**Findings:**

```
[DEMO-017]: Human Development composite trajectory not narrated — the human cost arc is absent
Severity: MEDIUM
Screenshot ref: frame-e (Zone 1D, Zone 1A)
What is shown: Zone 1D at step 4 shows framework scores. Financial recovery is narrated
  in the walkthrough. The HD composite trajectory (whether it improved, stagnated, or
  declined during the four-step arc) is not addressed in any walkthrough step.
What should be shown: The walkthrough should include at least one reference to the HD
  composite trajectory in the four-step arc. The finance minister's counterpart in a
  real negotiation is asking: "Did recovery reach ordinary Argentines?" The demo should
  name what the HD curve shows, even if it shows stagnation — that stagnation IS the
  human cost ledger output.
Recommendation: Add to walkthrough Step 7 (or Step 6 narration): "The Human Development
  composite [value/trend] — recovery of GDP does not automatically mean recovery of
  capability. The instrument panel makes this distinction visible." This is a narration
  change, not an implementation change. Closes the gap between the human cost ledger
  principle and the demo narrative.
```
