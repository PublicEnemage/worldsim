## Part 4 — North Star Assessment

**Scenario:** A Zambian Finance Ministry team of three economists has been told by the IMF: "This is the only viable fiscal adjustment path. Option A is the proposed programme. We have modelled the alternatives."

The ministry team has just run Demo 7 — Act 1 on their own Senegal reference case (testing whether any multiplier configuration avoids the bottom quintile threshold), Act 2 presenting the Zambia differential (+342,727 persons, T3, direction stable).

**Does Demo 7 change what the ministry team can argue at the table?**

**Act 1 (yes):** The ministry team can say: "We tested the FiscalMultiplier range between 0.5 and 2.0 on our Article IV reference case. At multiplier 0.85 — fifteen percent below the programme baseline — the bottom quintile poverty headcount at step 8 reads 0.450, above the 0.40 recovery floor. Under the programme's proposed multiplier assumption, the threshold is not structurally unavoidable. It is a function of the multiplier assumption." Frame C provides the on-screen evidence: CLEAR badge, 0.450, floor 0.40. The ministry team can point to the screen and say "this is not a political argument."

**Act 2 (yes):** The ministry team can say: "Under Option A, our simulation shows a poverty headcount differential of approximately 342,700 persons compared to Option C — the ministry's counter-proposal. The confidence interval is 298,000 to 398,000 at 95%. The direction is stable across the full range. Under T3 demographic weights from available Zambian population data, the sign does not flip. What does your distributional model show for the same comparison?"

That question — "what does your model show?" — changes the negotiating structure. The IMF team cannot dismiss a direction-stable T3 differential without disclosing their own distributional methodology. The ministry team is no longer asserting; they are demanding engagement.

**North Star verdict: PASSES.** Demo 7, as captured in run 12, demonstrates the primary mission capability — giving the finance ministry team something specific and citable to say back at the table. The Act 1 finding is readable from Frame C. The Act 2 differential is readable from Frames D/E with full methodology visible in Frame E.

The two HIGH findings (IR-M18-001 trajectory convergence, IR-M18-002 choropleth) are presentation quality issues, not argument integrity issues. The ministry team's arguments do not depend on the Zone 1A visual split in Frame A or on ZMB appearing in the choropleth. Resolving them improves the credibility of the demo context; failing to resolve them does not undermine the analytical standing the demo creates.
