## Demo Scenario Presented

### Act 1 — Senegal, Mode 3 Active Control

- **Country:** Senegal (SEN)
- **Context:** Article IV consultation, fiscal adjustment path under review
- **Mode:** Mode 3 Active Control — `ControlPlaneColumn` Form 1 with FiscalMultiplier slider
- **FiscalMultiplier tested:** 0.85 (15% below the programme baseline; within Ilzetzki et al.
  2013 SSA LIC consensus range 0.5–0.9)
- **Branch applied at:** Step 3 (Q3 2024)
- **Programme window:** 8 steps (Q1 2024 — Q4 2025)
- **Act 1 finding:** CLEAR at step 8 — Zone 1B reads 0.450 / floor 0.400. Threshold not
  crossed under the tested multiplier configuration.
- **Primary thesis:** Is there a fiscal multiplier configuration within the literature's consensus
  range that avoids the bottom quintile informal workers poverty headcount crossing the 0.40
  recovery floor before step 8?

### Act 2 — Zambia, Three-Scenario Comparison

- **Country:** Zambia (ZMB)
- **Context:** Debt restructuring — three competing programme proposals
- **Scenarios:** Option A (EFF Front-Loaded, IMF-proposed), Option B (EFF Gradual),
  Option C (Homegrown Programme, Ministry counter-proposal)
- **Reference scenario:** Option C (Ministry position — last in comparison list, Demo 7 convention)
- **Primary finding:** "Option A vs. Option C: +approximately 342,700 persons below poverty
  threshold · 298K–398K · 95% CI · T3 · Direction stable"
- **Act 2 claim:** The counter-proposal differential is a specific, tiered, directionally
  stable number — not a narrative assertion.

### Section 3 — Backtesting Credibility (3 minutes)

**Lucas** engages with each case in order. On Argentina: "−10.55% simulated against −10.9%

country with T3 cohort weights. That is what T3 means and the disclosure is correct — but
I want to make sure the ministry team understands the distinction between 'engine validated'
and 'country calibrated.'" He is not blocking this. He is naming it as the disclosure the
ministry team must be able to give when challenged.

**Eleni** responds to Greece 2010–2012. She says nothing during the presenter's narration —
she has worked in those rooms. After the presenter finishes, she speaks: "The direction was
not in dispute. Fiscal consolidation under external conditionality produced what the model
says it produces. The magnitude was the problem — not because models underestimated the
contraction, but because the human cost distribution was never in the models at all. The
spending cut trajectory was modelled. What happened to school attendance and clinic hours
was not." She pauses. "The human cost ledger you described — the four-framework architecture,
the cohort disaggregation in Zone 1B — that is the gap you are trying to close. That matters."

**Andreas** notes Ecuador: "The recovery case tells me the engine does not have a systematic

**Aicha** listens. She does not have a technical challenge on backtesting. She has a

### Section 4 — What Is Being Built (3 minutes)

**Lucas** hears the three roadmap items: empirically calibrated CI intervals (Bayesian
posterior from historical cohort data), SEN/ZMB backtesting, Mode 3 constraint floors.
He zeroes in on the constraint floors: "The current Mode 3 workflow — test one multiplier,
observe the result, adjust, test again — is analytically equivalent to manual parameter
search. The constraint-floor capability reverses the query: specify the human cost constraint
and let the instrument find the configuration that satisfies it. That is a much more powerful
tool than the current workflow. If that is on the M19 roadmap, it should be named as the
primary capability addition — not the CI calibration or the backtesting." He says this
directly. He is not criticizing Demo 7. He is identifying the next step.

**Aicha** hears the constraint-floor description and says the same thing in practitioner
terms: "In today's demonstration, the analyst moved the slider to 0.85 because the Step 5d
evaluation told you to. In a live negotiation, the analyst does not have a Step 5d
evaluation. The analyst needs to know: what is the multiplier range that avoids the crossing?
The constraint-floor capability gives the answer without a manual search. That is the
difference between a tool the analyst uses before the meeting and a tool the analyst can use
in the meeting." She looks at the presenter. "What is the timeline?"

**Andreas** focuses on the PSP driver decomposition roadmap. "The driver label in M18 tells
me the dominant category. What I need in M19 is the driver arc — how the dominant driver
changes across the eight steps of the programme. A political advisor briefs the minister on
trajectory, not on current state. If fiscal sustainability is dominant at step 3 and social
stability becomes dominant at step 6, that is a different political risk arc from one where
fiscal sustainability is dominant at every step. Show me that arc."

## Capability Gaps Named by Stakeholders

The following gaps were stated directly by stakeholders — not inferred, but named.

**Lucas Ferreira (P1 — IMF Senior Economist):**

1. **Mode 3 constraint-floor capability (named as primary M19 priority).** "The current
   workflow is manual parameter search. The constraint-floor capability reverses the query:
   specify the human cost constraint, let the instrument find the configuration space. That
   is the capability that changes the session dynamic from 'we tested this value' to 'here
   is the range that satisfies the constraint.'"

2. **Reproducibility path documentation.** "The Zone 3 panel is the first layer. The
   codebase is the complete layer. The walkthrough should include the repository URL and
   a note that reproducing the figure requires a matching scenario configuration — so
   a practitioner who is challenged in a session knows exactly what to say."

3. **CI band visibility in Mode 3.** "If the epistemic transparency argument is central,
   the uncertainty envelope must be perceptible in the thesis frame. The current 5%
   opacity in Mode 3 requires an explicit presenter direction to Frame B. Consider
   whether a slightly higher opacity (8–10%) would be visible at projection resolution
   without obscuring the trajectory split."

**Aicha Mbaye (P5 — Finance Minister, Senegal):**

4. **Mode 3 constraint-floor capability (named as the transition from 'tool before the
   meeting' to 'tool in the meeting').** "Today the analyst tests values one by one.
   The constraint floor makes the instrument ask: 'what configuration satisfies this
   threshold?' In the room, under twelve seconds, the analyst does not have time for
   manual search. The constraint floor closes that gap."

5. **Demonstration of the search workflow.** "The demo showed me the finding at 0.85.

   sequence is the demo argument made visceral. Show it live, not just the terminal state."

   is a snapshot. The arc — which driver was dominant at each step — is a trajectory.

   than 'fiscal sustainability at step 3.'"

was that Act 1's finding is on screen, not narrated. The CLEAR badge at 0.450/floor 0.400 in
Frame C is instrument output. Aicha does not need the presenter to say "the threshold is not
crossed." Zone 1B says it. That is the milestone that M14 was working toward. It is present
in M18.

— landed with the highest impact for Eleni and Lucas. Eleni accepted it as the format a
programme review session would require. Lucas accepted the direction-stability claim and
immediately identified the correct framing for citing it under challenge. Both acknowledged
the honest T3 and unbacktested-country disclosures as the right epistemic position.

The Zone 3 methodology panel expansion was the architectural moment. Lucas's reaction to Frame
E — "this is the first time I have seen a methodology panel expand without leaving the primary
surface" — is the validation the G5 delivery was designed to produce. The Panel closes the
analytical defence loop and was accepted by all four personas as the correct architecture for
handling the IMF team's methodology challenge.

### Most Compelling Moment

**Aicha's reaction to Frame C — the CLEAR badge.**

She had no verbal response. The finding was on screen. In M14, her verdict was: "In M15, if
Zone 1B states that sentence directly — without requiring the presenter to say it — I can

step 8 is the sentence on screen. She can forward Frame C. The presenter is not required to
be in the room for the finding to be legible.

The secondary compelling moment was Lucas's identification of Mode 3 constraint-floor as
the primary M19 priority — framed as the transition from manual parameter search to
instrument-driven configuration search. This is the first time the constraint-floor roadmap
item has been named by a domain economist as the specific capability that changes the
session dynamic from prepared demonstration to live instrument.

### Most Questioned Element

**Mode 3 constraint-floor capability — the gap between terminal-state demo and live search.**

Both Aicha and Lucas named this independently, from different analytical frames. The finding
at 0.85 is presented as a result. The process — slider movement, branch update, CROSSED/CLEAR
feedback — was not visible in the demo. Eleni and Aicha both asked to see the search sequence
in a future demonstration. This is not a criticism of Demo 7's architecture — the terminal
state is the correct thesis frame. It is a forward requirement: a live demonstration of the
Mode 3 search sequence would make the instrument's interactive capability visceral in a way
that a single frame at 0.85 does not.

The secondary most-questioned element was the CI band opacity in Mode 3. Eleni and Lucas
both noticed that the uncertainty envelope is marginal in Frame A. The presenter's resolution
(direct to Frame B) is correct, but the walkthrough should state this explicitly as a
presenter instruction.

The ministry's analyst opens Form 1 in Mode 3. FiscalMultiplier slider set to 0.85 (15%
below the programme baseline — within the Ilzetzki et al. 2013 SSA LIC consensus range
of 0.5–0.9). Presses Apply at step 3. The counter-trajectory branch runs forward from step

input log in the control column reads: "Step 3 — FiscalMultiplier: 0.85."

**The specific argument at the table:**

"Your programme embeds a fiscal multiplier assumption of 1.0. Under that assumption, the
bottom quintile informal workers poverty headcount crosses the 0.40 recovery floor before
programme completion. Under a multiplier of 0.85 — fifteen percent below your baseline, within
the empirical consensus range for an SSA low-income open economy — Zone 1B reads CLEAR at
programme completion. The threshold does not cross. The configuration is on record.

"We are not arguing that our multiplier assumption is correct. We are demonstrating that the
threshold crossing is a function of the multiplier assumption embedded in your proposed
conditionality — not a structural consequence of the adjustment itself. The Ilzetzki et al.
2013 consensus for SSA LIC economies places the fiscal multiplier between 0.5 and 0.9.
Your programme baseline sits above that range. That is the contestable element.

"What does your distributional model show at multiplier 0.85?"

Yes — and for the first time, unconditionally.

circulate before the meeting, submit during the meeting, or include in a written submission

This is the specific change from M14: the ministry team's counter-proposal argument no longer
requires a domain economist or a presenter to interpret the output in the room. A finance
minister or a policy advisor can read Frame C and state the finding. The gap Aicha named in
M14 is closed.

No conditions. The capability is on screen. The finding is in the screenshot. The ministry
