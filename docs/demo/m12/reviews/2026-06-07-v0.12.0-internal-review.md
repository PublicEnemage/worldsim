## Agent Panel Findings

### Finding DEMO4-001 — Reserves Frozen (CRITICAL) — RESOLVED

**Status:** RESOLVED — PR #798 merged 2026-06-07.

**Root cause:** `ExternalSectorModule` emitted `import_price_inflation` events but no module
consumed them to produce `reserve_coverage_months` changes. The attribute was seeded at
scenario start and never modified.

**Fix:** `ExternalSectorModule` now emits a third event per commodity shock with
`affected_attributes={"reserve_coverage_months": reserve_delta}` where
`reserve_delta = -(dep × shock × _RESERVE_BURN_RATE)` with `_RESERVE_BURN_RATE = 8.5`.
This is a FLOW-type delta (additive) so it reduces reserves each step the shock is active.

**Verification:** JOR reserves trace 7.1→6.2→5.0→3.7→2.5→1.2→0.0→0.0 over 8 steps.
JOR CRITICAL MDA fires at step 5 (2.459mo < 2.5 floor), matching the screenshot brief
Frame E requirement ("Peak alert density — reserve CRITICAL for JOR").

**Near-miss filed:** NM-038.

---

### Finding DEMO4-002 — Unemployment Frozen (CRITICAL) — RESOLVED

**Status:** RESOLVED — PR #798 merged 2026-06-07.

**Root cause:** No Okun's law channel existed connecting GDP changes to country-level
`unemployment_rate`. `MacroeconomicModule` computed GDP deltas but emitted no downstream
unemployment event.

**Fix:** `MacroeconomicModule` now emits a separate `unemployment_rate_change` event with
`Quantity(value=-OKUN_COEFFICIENT × gdp_delta)` where `OKUN_COEFFICIENT = Decimal("0.5")`
(Ball et al. 2019 / Loayza-Raddatz 2010 developing-economy estimate). Full calibration
deferred to Issue #44.

**Verification:** JOR unemployment 17.77%→17.75%→17.73%→17.71%→18.26%→18.19%→18.13%→18.07%.
The fiscal cut at step 4 (−3% spending, one-step lag) causes unemployment to jump +0.55pp
at step 5 — visible analytical signal. Subsequent steps show partial recovery via mean reversion.

**Near-miss filed:** NM-038.

### Finding DEMO4-003 — GDP Rising Monotonically Under Compound Crisis (MEDIUM) — PARTIALLY RESOLVED

**Status:** PARTIALLY RESOLVED — IMF austerity creates visible GDP dip at step 5 (+1.58%
vs +2.67% at step 4). However, steps 1–4 show monotonically rising GDP despite commodity
shock, and steps 6–8 show steady recovery. The commodity shock itself does not depress GDP
because `ExternalSectorModule` events (`import_price_inflation`) are not subscribed to by
`MacroeconomicModule`.

**Analytical note:** The mean-reversion channel (ADR-006 Amendment 1) dominates steps 1–4
because JOR's `trend_growth` (3.0%) > `current_gdp_growth` (2.5%). The commodity shock
has no GDP transmission channel currently. Full commodity-shock → GDP linkage (MacroeconomicModule
subscribing to `commodity_price_shock_*` events) is out of scope for M12 — deferred to M13.

**Demo impact:** GDP rising while reserves drain is analytically coherent (Jordan has maintained
positive GDP growth despite real balance-of-payments pressures historically). The dip at step 5
is the primary signal. MEDIUM severity — does not block screenshot capture.

**Issue to file:** Commodity shock → GDP transmission channel (MacroeconomicModule extension).
