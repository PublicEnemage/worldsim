## Demo 6 — Senegalese Finance Minister (Distributional Human Cost)

**Primary argument:** A finance minister can now show not just the aggregate trajectory
but *who bears the cost* — distributional disaggregation by cohort surfaces which
populations are hit hardest and when. This is the argument that changes what can be
said at the table.

**What is new in Milestone 16:**

1. **Zone 1A Phase 4 — Composite encoding** — The primary viewport now shows
   distributional signals at the Zone 1A surface level. The composite encoding is
   the first time the cohort-level signals are visible in the primary instrument without
   opening a secondary panel.

2. **Cohort disaggregation on primary surface (#986)** — The instrument cluster
   presents distributional effects by demographic cohort alongside aggregate financial
   indicators. The ministry team can now show that fiscal conditionality lands
   disproportionately on specific cohorts — not just on aggregate GDP.

3. **Political risk summary (#987)** — Zone 1D now includes a political risk summary
   surface, quantifying programme implementation feasibility under the current
   political economy conditions.

## Section 1 — The Room

There is a room where this happens. On one side: creditor team with institutional memory
and proprietary models. On the other: finance ministry, three economists, public data.

The question is never "do you have a model?" The question is: "Can you show that your
number is correct — and can you show who bears the cost of the alternative?"

Today the ministry team can answer both questions.

## Section 3 — Backtesting Credibility

Five validated historical cases: Greece, Lebanon, Thailand, Ecuador, Zambia.
DIRECTION_ONLY on all five. Ecuador year 1: MAGNITUDE calibrated.
The distributional disaggregation is not yet backtested against historical cohort data —
honest disclosure. The aggregate trajectory is calibrated; the cohort distribution is
scenario output under the configured demographic weights.

## Section 4 — What Is Being Built

M16 delivers two capabilities demonstrated today:
- **Distributional visibility:** Cohort disaggregation on the primary surface (#986).
- **Political risk summary:** Programme implementation feasibility (#987).

What comes next (M17): full Mode 3 Active Control with distributional constraints;
counter-proposal branch that explicitly names which cohort the ministry is protecting.

*There is a quinoa farmer in Bolivia who will never know this tool exists.
His government might. If his government has a finance minister with better analytical
tools, that minister can negotiate better terms. The quinoa farmer lives at the end
of that chain. Build it as if he does.*
