### Finding DEMO6-010 — `valueDisplay` Label "above floor" Is Semantically Inverted (MEDIUM)

**Agent:** Chief Methodologist / Development Economist

**Status:** MEDIUM — stakeholders may notice; does not block live demo if presenter can clarify.

**Description:**

Each cohort crossing entry in Zone 1B displays a distance field rendered as:
`${crossing.above_floor_pct}% above floor`

The `above_floor_pct` field is computed as:
```python
pct_below = ((floor_val - ind_val) / floor_val * 100)  # positive when ind_val < floor_val
```

For a cohort at poverty_headcount_ratio = 0.385 with recovery floor 0.40:
`pct_below = (0.40 - 0.385) / 0.40 × 100 = 3.75`

The UI renders: **"3.75% above floor"**

The label "above floor" implies the cohort has exceeded the floor in a positive direction. However, the cohort is 3.75% BELOW the recovery floor (poverty headcount is lower than the floor — which may be good or a breach, depending on threshold semantics). The label is at minimum ambiguous and at worst inverted.

For a stakeholder audience that understands the recovery floor as the minimum sustainable threshold, "3.75% above floor" will read as "this cohort is safely above the threshold" — the opposite of the crisis signal the demo intends to convey.

**Fix required (two options):**

Option 1 (label fix, no backend change): Change `valueDisplay` label to `below floor` in the frontend:
```typescript
const valueDisplay = isSad ? "—" : (crossing.above_floor_pct != null ? `${crossing.above_floor_pct}% below floor` : "—");
```

Option 2 (rename field): Rename `above_floor_pct` → `pct_below_floor` in `CohortThresholdCrossing` schema and all references, and update label accordingly. Cleaner long-term but wider diff.

**Recommendation:** Option 1 for Demo 6 (label-only fix); Option 2 at M17 schema cleanup.
