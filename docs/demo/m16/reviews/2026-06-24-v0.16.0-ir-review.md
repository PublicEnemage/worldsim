## Part 2 — Domain Expert Legibility and Usefulness

### Zone 1B CohortImpactSection

The Zone 1B panel is the most analytically important element in Demo 6. The walkthrough's thesis — "that is not a political claim, that is a citeable technical finding" — depends entirely on Zone 1B being legible and specific. At presentation scale, the text rows are too small for a room audience at normal projection distance to read the specific cohort identifier, current value, floor label, or T3 Inferred badge without zoom. The walkthrough's "PAUSE — let them read Zone 1B and the T3 badge" instruction assumes Zone 1B is readable at room scale. The screenshot evidence does not support this.

**Citability verdict:** Panel contents may be citable in a follow-up session with the analyst's own laptop. In a live projected demo, the T3 Inferred badge is not legible as the visual anchor the narration requires.

### Zone 1A Composite Encoding

The composite encoding is meant to show four framework curves diverging as conditionality begins. At current rendering scale and calibration, the curves appear flat and overlapping. The Human Development curve — the signal the demo claims to surface — is not labeled in the visible legend. A non-technical stakeholder would require the presenter to point and identify the curve, which is annotation, not legibility. An instrument that requires a human pointer to identify its most important element has not met the "readable at a glance" design specification.

### Zone 1D PSP Surface

The PSP value of 0.71 raises a calibration question: the walkthrough notes the initial legitimacy index is ~0.43 placing PSP "in WARNING zone at programme entry," but PSP and legitimacy index are distinct measures. A domain economist will immediately ask "what does 0.71 mean?" — the severity label exists to answer this, but it is not legible at room scale. The plain-language interpretation sentence is the right design element. If legible at room scale it translates PSP into briefing-note vocabulary. The screenshot evidence does not confirm legibility.

### 25-Year Projection Panel

The panel is not rendered in any frame. The "IN YOUR PREDICTION" placeholder text is a production-readiness signal to any technical reviewer in the room. The "for this long" argument — the intergenerational claim that conditionality drives cohorts below structural recovery thresholds beyond the programme window — cannot be made without the panel. This is the most distinctive M16 visual and its absence from every frame is the single highest-priority finding.

### T3 Confidence Tier as Epistemic Honesty vs. Adversarial Tool

The T3 Inferred badge is genuinely the right design choice — hiding the inference would undermine credibility the moment the source is challenged. However, the Q&A preparation has a specific gap (IR-009): a creditor team that understands T3 methodology can use the ±0.05 uncertainty band to argue that 0.385 is not demonstrably below 0.40 — the confidence interval encompasses the floor. The Q&A preparation treats the T3 challenge as "can you trust the number?" rather than "can you establish threshold proximity given your uncertainty bounds?" The PR #1241 walkthrough update added the ±0.05 band and the reframe toward "what does your distributional model show?" — but the specific counter ("your band encompasses the floor") needs a prepared response. The response options: (a) analysis is not claiming crossing has occurred, only that the cohort is in the vicinity — trajectory direction matters regardless of whether the point estimate is ±0.05; (b) the ±0.05 is the outer bound and the ECOWAS distribution's modal estimate is tighter.

## Shared Root Cause Analysis

Three distinct shared root causes explain the majority of findings:

**Root Cause 1 — Engine calibration produces insufficient visual signal at demo scale (IR-001, IR-005, IR-008)**
The fiscal-to-human-development elasticity (~+0.0015pp per step) produces ~0.012pp of change over eight steps — below the visual threshold for the trajectory chart at current scale. The same calibration constraint means the milestone sentence may not fire (IR-008) because the crossing threshold is not reached within the demo window. The PSP reading of 0.71 (IR-005) may reflect a political degradation rate that does not produce WARNING within the eight-step demo window. These three findings share a single underlying cause: the demo scenario's analytical claims may outrun the engine's calibration at the specified step count and elasticity settings.

**Root Cause 2 — HumanCapitalTrajectoryPanel not rendered to demo-ready state (IR-002, IR-008, IR-011)**
The panel renders placeholder text across all five frames. This eliminates the 25-year visual entirely (IR-002), removes the milestone sentence from all frames (IR-008), and makes Frame D indistinguishable from the others (IR-011). This is the highest-priority single finding — the most distinctive M16 visual is not on screen in any frame.

**Root Cause 3 — Demo assets not validated at presentation scale (IR-003, IR-004, IR-006, IR-010)**
Frame B=C identical (accepted by EL), Zone 1B text too small (IR-004), PSP severity label not visible (IR-006), and delta annotation not visible (IR-010) share a common cause: screenshots captured without validating legibility at room projection scale. The brief may have specified content without verifying the captured content is visible and differentiated at presentation distance.

## North Star Assessment

**Scenario:** Senegal 2024 Article IV consultation. A Senegalese finance ministry team of three economists facing a creditor team with institutional programme history, asked in real time: "Which cohort bears the cost of this conditionality, and can you cite that?"

**Analytical capability demonstrated:** Cohort-specific distributional disaggregation (Zone 1B), composite framework encoding (Zone 1A), political economy constraint labeling (Zone 1D), 25-year structural consequence projection — all on one screen, without specialist mediation.

**Does the capability change what the ministry team can argue at the table?**

The architecture would — but the demo does not demonstrate it as currently screenshotted.

The designed capability, if fully implemented and calibrated, represents a genuine shift in negotiating standing. The cohort identifier, T3 Inferred badge, ECOWAS source citation, and the ability to ask "what does your distributional model show for this cohort?" are real tools for changing the character of a negotiation. The walkthrough's Q&A section understands this correctly: it is not about matching the IMF's precision, it is about putting a named, sourced, citable distributional claim on the table from the ministry's own analytical infrastructure.

However, the five demo frames as currently captured do not demonstrate this capability:
1. The Zone 1A composite encoding does not show the divergence the narration claims (IR-001). A presenter saying "the human development decline is visible in Zone 1A" while the audience sees flat lines damages credibility by Frame B.
2. The 25-year trajectory panel is absent (IR-002). The "for this long" argument — the intergenerational claim — is not on screen.
3. Frame D is indistinguishable from the preceding frames (IR-011), eliminating the structural consequence beat from the demo sequence.

A domain economist or programme director in that room will be charitable for two frames. By Frame C, when the presenter is describing something the audience cannot see, the question shifts from "is this tool useful?" to "is this tool ready?"

**North Star verdict: DOES NOT PASS in current screenshotted state.** The architectural design is correct. The narration is strong. The Q&A preparation is largely sound. The tool, if the panel renders and the calibration produces visible signal, would change what the ministry team can argue. But three of the five demo frames do not show what they are narrated to show, and the most distinctive M16 feature is absent from every frame.
