### Calibration state (as documented in EL decision 2026-06-24)

The fiscal-to-cohort-poverty transmission chain produces ~+0.0015pp Q1 poverty delta per
active conditionality step (standard multiplier 0.5 × −0.030 spend = −0.015pp GDP growth
change × −0.10 elasticity from Lustig 2017). Starting from 0.385, crossing the 0.40 MDA
floor within the 8-step programme window requires ~+0.015pp total — approximately 10 steps,
not achievable within n_steps=8. The `imf_program_acceptance` event has no direct
demographic transmission pathway in the current ELASTICITY_REGISTRY. Filed as Issue #1229.

### Finding DEMO6-001 — Spec TTS Narration Frame A Claims Zone 1B Crossing That Does Not Exist (CRITICAL)

**Agent:** Business PO / Independent Review Agent

**Status:** MUST FIX before any external presentation or recording.

**Description:**

The demo spec TTS narration at Frame A (Step 2) states:

> *"The cohort impact section shows: bottom quintile informal workers poverty headcount.
> Current value: at or above zero point four zero. Recovery floor: zero point four zero.
> This cohort is at the threshold. Six months in."*

At step 2 with the current calibration, the Q1 cohort poverty headcount is ~0.388 —
approaching but not crossing the 0.40 MDA floor. `CohortImpactSection` renders based on
`cohort_threshold_crossings` from the store. When no cohort has crossed the floor, the
section renders: *"No cohort threshold crossings at or before this step."*

The narration says "at or above zero point four zero." The screen shows "No cohort threshold
crossings at or before this step." These are directly contradictory.

**Root cause:** The `stakeholder-walkthrough.md §Frame A` was correctly updated per EL
direction (2026-06-24) to the honest "approaching the floor" framing. The spec TTS narration
was not updated before screenshots were captured. The walkthrough (live presenter path) is
honest; the spec narration (automated recording path) is not.

**Demo impact:** BLOCKING for any recorded or automated presentation. A live presenter
using the walkthrough is honest. A recording of the automated spec would speak factual
claims that do not match the screen. The "No False Precision" founding principle prohibits
stating a value as fact when the screen shows otherwise.

**Fix required:**
Update Frame A TTS narration in the spec to match the walkthrough §Frame A:
```typescript
await speak(
  "Step two. Q2 2024. Six months into the programme. " +
  "Fiscal conditionality has begun: social spending cut by three percent of GDP. " +
  "Look at Zone one B — the alert and cohort panel. " +
  "At programme entry, the bottom quintile informal workers poverty headcount is at zero point three eight five — " +
  "approaching the recovery floor at zero point four zero. " +
  "The badge next to that trajectory reads T3 — Inferred. " +
  "ECOWAS comparable economy distributions. " +
  "This is the threshold argument: not aggregate poverty — this specific cohort, at this step, " +
  "closing on the floor that triggers a decade of capability loss.",
);
```

### Finding DEMO6-002 — Frame A and Frame D Identical; Frame B and Frame C Identical (CRITICAL)

**Agent:** UX Designer / QA Lead

**Status:** MUST FIX before external demo artifacts (deck, recording) are produced.

**Description:**

The demo spec captures Frame A and Frame D both at step 2 with no scrolling, state change,
or viewport modification between the two `page.screenshot()` calls. The resulting PNG files
are byte-for-byte identical (248,179 bytes each). Similarly, Frame B and Frame C are both
captured at step 4 with no interaction between them (249,978 bytes each).

The screenshot brief specifies five *distinct* frames with different compositional focuses:
- Frame A: Zone 1B CohortImpactSection as compositional center
- Frame D: HumanCapitalTrajectoryPanel as compositional center
- Frame B: Zone 1A composite encoding as compositional center
- Frame C: Zone 1D PSP severity label as compositional center

At 1440×900, all instruments and the projection panel are simultaneously visible
(AC-F6 non-displacement confirmed). This is the demo's thesis: *everything on one screen*.
But identical screenshots cannot communicate different compositional focuses in a slide deck
or async review. A reviewer who sees Frame A and Frame D side by side sees the same image twice.

This is the same failure mode as M14 DEMO-102 (Frames B and C identical). The root cause
recurred because the spec was written with the assumption that different panels would be in
different scroll positions or viewport states, but the non-displacement design means they
co-exist at the same scroll position.

**Demo impact:** HIGH for async materials (slide deck, documentation). LOW for live demo
(presenter can point to different areas of the same image verbally). For the Step 9 live
stakeholder session (#843), the presenter can navigate the live app rather than relying on
the screenshot deck — so this does not block the live demo itself.

**Fix required (three options, in order of preference):**

Option 1 (immediate, no spec change): Annotate the screenshots in the demo deck with
labeled callout boxes — Frame A with a red box around Zone 1B, Frame D with a blue box
around the projection panel. The identical screenshots become two *annotated views* of the
same surface. This preserves the "all on one screen" thesis.

Option 2 (spec change): Add CSS highlight overlays between captures using Playwright
`page.evaluate()` to temporarily highlight the relevant panel, then capture, then remove.
This produces distinguishable frames without scrolling.

Option 3 (spec change): Accept that this demo has 3 visually distinct frames (step 2,
step 4, step 8) and rename accordingly. Remove duplicate filenames and update the brief.

**Immediate recommendation:** Option 1 for the live demo (#843). File Option 2 or 3
as an improvement for M18.

### Finding DEMO6-005 — Zone 1B "Approaching" Is Not the Same Argument as "Crossing" — Presenter Must Know the Distinction (HIGH)

**Agent:** Political Economist / Geopolitical Analyst

**Status:** HIGH — requires explicit presenter note; does not block the live demo.

**Description:**

The walkthrough §What They Need to Leave Believing includes:
> *"1. The tool shows the cost is distributional — a specific cohort, approaching the
> recovery floor, is named."*

The live demo argument has been correctly updated to "approaching" (EL direction 2026-06-24).
However, in a sophisticated audience (finance ministry, academic economists, IMF staff), the
distinction between "approaching" and "crossing" matters. A creditor will probe: *"Your T3
estimate shows 0.385. What's the confidence interval? Could the actual value already be above
0.40?"*

The honest answer is: yes, it could. T3 Inferred means the estimate derives from ECOWAS
regional comparable economy distributions, not Senegal-specific microdata. The T3 uncertainty
band encompasses values both below and above the threshold.

**Presenter preparation required:**

The presenter must be briefed on TWO versions of the Zone 1B argument:
1. If a crossing IS visible: "This cohort has crossed the recovery floor at step N. That
   is the tool's finding, at T3 confidence."
2. If the section shows "No crossings": "The T3 estimate at programme entry is 0.385 —
   approaching the 0.40 floor. The 25-year trajectory shows this cohort closing on that
   threshold under the proposed conditionality. The distributional argument stands regardless
   of whether the step-level crossing is computable within the programme window."

The T3 badge is the credibility anchor in both cases. It does not hide the uncertainty — it
declares it precisely: *"Here is what we know, at T3. Here is what T3 means."*

> already be approaching or above 0.40. The T3 badge is not a hedge. It is a precise
> statement about what we know and what we inferred. We would rather declare the inference
> than present a falsely precise number.'"*

## North Star Test (Sprint Exit Artifact)

*Required by CLAUDE.md §North Star Test (Process Gate). Authored by Business PO.*

**Finance minister scenario:** A Senegalese finance ministry analyst is in a pre-negotiation
briefing the morning before the Article IV consultation. The IMF team's paper proposes a
social spending cut of 3% of GDP as fiscal conditionality. The analyst's job is to produce
a quantified distributional argument before the session opens.

**Capability being evaluated:** Demo 6 — Zone 1B cohort disaggregation, Zone 1A composite
encoding, Zone 1D political risk summary, 25-year human capital trajectory.

**Does this capability change what the ministry team can argue at the table?**

With the current calibration state (Issue #1229 outstanding), the answer is conditional:

- **Argument the tool DOES enable:** "At programme entry, the bottom quintile informal
  workers poverty headcount is at 0.385 — approaching the 0.40 recovery floor. Under the
  proposed conditionality, the 25-year trajectory shows this cohort closing on that threshold.
  The precision of who bears the cost is now visible: bottom quintile, informal sector,
  working age. We can cite the ECOWAS T3 estimate and declare the uncertainty." This argument
  **is** citeable at the table with the T3 badge visible. The analyst no longer needs to
  say "the poor" — they can say "this cohort."

- **Argument the tool DOES NOT yet enable:** "At step 2, this cohort crosses the recovery
  floor. Capability restoration takes a decade or more." This requires calibrated
  fiscal-to-cohort poverty elasticity (Issue #1229, M17 scope).

**North Star test verdict:** The tool is partially mission-ready for Demo 6. The approach
trajectory and distributional disaggregation argument IS available and IS citable. The
crossing argument IS NOT available and must NOT be claimed in the session. The walkthrough
has been correctly updated to reflect this. The spec TTS narration has not (blocking fix
