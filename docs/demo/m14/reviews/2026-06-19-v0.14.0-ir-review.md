## Part 1 — Narration vs. Visual Match

### Frame C — "The Citation at the Table" (presented first, THESIS)

**Walkthrough claims:** Step 3, 2026; reserve 2.9 months; Zone 1B shows "Moderate confidence — cite
with caveat" AND the Layer 3 sentence "CRITICAL floor is one step away"; Grounding strip open with
T2 · IMF WEO Apr 2024 citation simultaneously visible.

**Screenshot confirms:**
- Step 3 of 6 ✓
- Reserve 2.908 months in Zone 1B ✓
- "Moderate confidence — cite with caveat" ✓
- Grounding strip open with source citations ✓

**Screenshot contradicts:**
- Zone 1B text after the tier label reads: "16.3% above floor at step 3. 0 consecutive steps"
  — NOT "at the current draw rate, the CRITICAL floor is one step away"
- The walkthrough's own reference table caption for Frame B/C states the expected Zone 1B
  content as: "current 2.9 / floor 2.5 / 'CRITICAL floor is one step away'" — this text is
  absent from the screenshot

**Verdict: PARTIAL MATCH.** The structural elements (step, value, tier label, Grounding strip)
match. The Layer 3 self-interpreting sentence — the capability the demo is named around —
does not match. The screenshot shows raw computational outputs where the brief specifies
human-interpretable analysis.

## Part 2 — Domain Expert UX Assessment

**The Grounding strip concept resonates immediately.** "IMF WEO · 2024-Q1 · T2" next to
"Reserve coverage: 3.8 months" is the citation format I'd need at the table. Readable in under
five seconds. The architecture is genuinely useful. This lands.

**"0 consecutive steps" stops me cold.** I do not know what this means without asking. Is this
zero consecutive steps the indicator has been in breach? Zero steps until breach? Zero consecutive
steps in the WARNING zone? I would interrupt the presenter here to ask. That is a demo-killing
moment — this tool is supposed to make specialist mediation unnecessary, and this text requires
exactly that mediation.

**The Layer 3 self-interpreting sentence I was promised is not what I see.** The walkthrough says
Zone 1B delivers "the self-interpreting sentence: at the current draw rate, the CRITICAL floor is
reached within one step." That sentence would be exactly what I need in a negotiation — I can put
it in a briefing note verbatim. What I see instead is "16.3% above floor at step 3. 0 consecutive
steps." These are not the same thing. The former tells me what to DO with the information. The
latter is two raw calculations that require interpretation. This is the difference between a Layer 3
output and a Layer 2 output. The demo's central claim cannot be demonstrated if this is what Zone 1B
actually shows.

**Dual reserve values create immediate confusion.** At Step 3, the Grounding strip shows 3.8 months
(no label indicating this is the initial value) while Zone 1B shows 2.908 months. Without narration,
a domain economist who has never seen WorldSim reads two values for the same indicator and asks
"which one is the current reserve coverage?" The panel needs a visible "Initial state (2024-Q1)"
header so the distinction is self-evident.

**The PSP concept resonates. The PSP value does not reach me.** I understand the argument — reserve
stress and programme viability are distinct but linked constraints. That framing is accurate and will
land with institutional audiences. But I cannot clearly read the PSP numeric value in Frame D at the
resolution shown. A claim about quantified programme survival probability that I cannot verify from
the screenshot is an assertion, not evidence.

**Frame E is the weakest frame despite being the closing argument.** The evidence thread claim — "the
ministry team can defend every number at the table without specialist mediation" — should be the most
visually confident moment of the demonstration. Instead it is the most compressed and the hardest to
read. The choropleth at the bottom of the screen redirects my attention to geography when the argument
lives in Zone 1.

## What Worked

The trust architecture's structural concept is entirely clear and persuasive from Frame A alone.
Seeing source institution, confidence tier, and vintage date in the scenario header before any analysis
runs is a genuine capability that no domain economist has seen from a public tool. The Grounding strip
is the right instrument for the challenge-response moment.

"Moderate confidence — cite with caveat" as a negotiation-defensibility label is immediately useful
to a practitioner. I know exactly what to do with that label in a briefing.

The scenario configuration (ZMB, food shock, conditionality, single entity) is analytically credible.
The reserve depletion arc (3.8 → 2.9 at step 3) is a realistic trajectory for this programme type.

The PSP architecture — programme survival probability as a distinct dimension from financial composites —
is the most analytically differentiated claim in the demo. If the number is readable and the model logic
is sound, this will be the question the audience asks most about in Q&A. That is a good problem to have.

The honest disclosures framework (Zambia not yet backtested, ecological module disabled, PSP
exploratory in M14) is exactly the right epistemic posture for an audience of domain economists.
Credibility comes from being honest about limitations before being asked.

## What Confused or Misled

1. "0 consecutive steps" — opaque jargon, no context, no label, would generate an interrupting
   question in a live demo.

2. Dual reserve values (3.8 in Grounding strip, 2.908 in Zone 1B) without an "Initial state" label
   on the Grounding strip panel — a first-time viewer reads two current values, not an initial value
   plus a current value.

3. Zone 1A Y-axis says "Score" — the presenter narrates "the reserve trajectory" as if reserve months
   are readable from the chart. A practitioner trying to find 3.8 → 2.9 months on a 0.00–0.80 score
   axis will not find it. The reserve months are only in Zone 1B.

4. Frames B and C identical — when the audience is shown the same screenshot for the thesis frame and
   then again as the "Zone 1B detail" frame, they will notice. Presenting the same visual twice while
   narrating two different arguments undermines presenter credibility.

5. The layout shift from Frames A/B/C (Grounding strip open, no choropleth) to Frames D/E (full
   viewport with choropleth) is jarring. The audience will spend cognitive capacity reorienting to the
   interface rather than processing the political economy argument.

## Root Cause Analysis

**Root Cause A — Layer 3 implementation gap (IR-001, IR-002, IR-004):**
Zone 1B generates raw computational outputs (percentage above floor, consecutive-steps-in-breach
count) rather than the trajectory-directional sentence the ADR-015 brief specifies. The L0 annotation
on Zone 1A trajectories was specified as zero-interaction visible but is either absent or below the
legibility threshold in all five screenshots. The demo's central capability claim ("Zone 1B is Layer 3
— it tells you what the number means, not just what it is") is contradicted by what the screenshots
actually show. This is the highest-priority finding: it is not a narration correction, it is an
implementation gap.

**Root Cause B — Viewport and capture configuration (IR-003, IR-007, IR-009):**
The capture specification produced identical screenshots for Frames B and C (same step, same state, no
viewport difference), and used a full-viewport layout for Frames D and E that compresses Zone 1 content
and introduces the choropleth as a visual competitor. Frame B was specified to show Step 2 (3.2 months)
in the screenshot brief; both B and C ended up at Step 3 because the WARNING alert does not fire at Step 2.
The brief was not updated to specify a viewport differentiation between the two Step-3 frames. This is a
capture specification problem, not an application problem — but it directly affects five of the ten
findings.

IR-005 (Grounding strip disambiguation) is a standalone design gap: the panel correctly shows
initial-state data, but without a visible "Initial state (2024-Q1)" header on the panel itself, a
first-time viewer at Step 3 sees two values for the same indicator with no visual signal about which
is current.

IR-006 and IR-008 are label-narration consistency mismatches — "2024-Q1" vs. "April 2024," and
"Score" vs. "reserve months" — both correctable in the UI labels or the narration script.

## Blocking Items for Next Demo

The following must be resolved before the stakeholder demo:

1. **IR-001 (CRITICAL):** Confirm whether the Layer 3 trajectory-directional sentence
   ("CRITICAL floor is one step away" or equivalent) is being generated by Zone 1B, and whether it is
   visible at 1440×900 at presentation distance. If the sentence is absent from the implementation or
   below the legibility threshold, the demo's central capability claim cannot be made honestly.

2. **IR-002 (HIGH):** Remove or relabel "0 consecutive steps" — replace with interpretable language
   or remove if it does not serve the canonical user.

3. **IR-003 (HIGH):** Differentiate Frames B and C — either use a closer crop of Zone 1B for Frame B,
   or collapse to four frames and adjust the walkthrough narration sequence.

4. **IR-004 (HIGH):** Verify L0 tier badge renders on Zone 1A trajectory curve at 1440×900 at
   projector distance. If not legible, either fix the annotation size or remove the narration claim
   about the badge.

5. **IR-005 (HIGH):** Add an "Initial state" label to the Grounding strip panel header so the 3.8-month
   value is self-evidently the starting value, not a competing current reading.
