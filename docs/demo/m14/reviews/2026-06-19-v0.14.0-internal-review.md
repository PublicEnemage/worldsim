### Finding DEMO5-001 — Walkthrough Thesis Frame at Wrong Step (CRITICAL)

**Agent:** Political Economist / Council Orchestrator

**Status:** MUST FIX before stakeholder demo.

**Description:**

`stakeholder-walkthrough.md §Step 2 — Frame C: The Citation at the Table` is titled
"(Step 2, 2025)" and its opening narration reads: "Step two. Year 2025. The IMF
program's fiscal conditionality begins. Reserve coverage is declining. This is the moment.
The creditor says: 'Where does your 3.8 months figure come from?'"

The walkthrough then describes "Zone 1B persistent-detail shows the reserve coverage
alert with full Layer 3 output."

**Root cause:** With the configured reserve depletion arc, the reserve WARNING alert does
not fire until step 3 (2026), when reserve dips to 2.908 months below the 3.0 WARNING
threshold. At step 2 (2025), reserve is ~3.354 months — above the WARNING floor. Zone 1B
at step 2 will show "No active threshold breaches." The thesis moment cannot be delivered
at step 2 because the alert is not there.

**Demo impact:** BLOCKING. A presenter following the walkthrough will advance to step 2
for the thesis moment and see an empty Zone 1B. The entire challenge-response thesis fails.

**Fix required:** Update walkthrough §Step 2 — Frame C to reflect step 3 (2026). The
fiscal conditionality note (step 2 spending cut) should remain as context but the challenge-
response moment should be moved to step 3 where the alert is actually live.

The spec narration is correct: "Step three. Year 2026. Two years into the programme."

### Finding DEMO5-003 — Zone 1B Tier Label Narration Does Not Match Screen (HIGH)

**Agent:** Chief Methodologist

**Status:** MUST FIX before stakeholder demo.

**Description:**

`stakeholder-walkthrough.md §Step 2 — Frame C` narrates:

> "Zone 1B — the alert panel — shows: Reserve Coverage (months). The tier label:
> **T2 · IMF WEO Apr 2024**."

Screenshot confirms Zone 1B shows: "Moderate confidence — cite with caveat" as the
negotiation-defensibility label.

**Root cause:** The walkthrough conflates two display elements that are architecturally
distinct:
- **Grounding strip:** Shows initial input provenance — "T2 · IMF WEO Apr 2024" for
  reserve_coverage_months at step 0. This is visible in Frame C.
- **Zone 1B alert:** Shows the negotiation-defensibility label for the *computed* reserve
  at step 3. This value is derived using food_import_dependency_food (T3) and initial
  reserve (T2). Output tier = T3 (max of inputs = lower confidence). T3 label = "Moderate
  confidence — cite with caveat."

The "T2 · IMF WEO Apr 2024" text does appear on screen — in the Grounding strip — but
not in Zone 1B. A presenter who says "Zone 1B shows: T2 · IMF WEO Apr 2024" is pointing
at the wrong element. A domain economist will notice.

**Fix required:** Correct §Step 2 — Frame C narration to distinguish the two elements:
- Zone 1B shows: "Reserve Coverage (months). Moderate confidence — cite with caveat."
  (T3, because the computed output uses T3 food import dependency)
- The Grounding strip shows: "T2 · IMF WEO Apr 2024" for the initial 3.8-month input.
- Both are simultaneously visible, which is the key composition of Frame C.

The distinction is analytically significant and honest: the initial input is T2, but
the computed trajectory value incorporates T3 data and the tool labels it accordingly.
The No False Precision principle in action. The narration should name this.

### Finding DEMO5-006 — Human Cost Ledger Absent from All Five Presentation Steps (MEDIUM)

**Agent:** Development Economist / Intergenerational Advocate / Community Resilience

**Status:** Does not block demo; accepted as scope limitation for Demo 5. Filed for M15
scope obligation.

**Development Economist assessment:**

The food commodity shock (magnitude 0.35) is the primary driver of reserve depletion in
this scenario. A food price shock of this magnitude — especially in Zambia, a net food
importer with 64% rural population — produces direct impacts on nutritional status,
caloric intake, and food expenditure share for bottom-quintile households. The
`bottom_quintile_consumption_capacity` output is being computed by the ExternalSectorModule
but is not narrated in any of the five presentation steps.

The demo's thesis is trust architecture, not distributional analysis. That framing
justifies the scope. However, there is a missed opportunity at Frame E: when the
presenter closes the "evidence thread" narrative, one sentence connecting reserve depletion
to food security impact would land the mission statement without adding analytical
complexity. "The reserve draw measures a financial stress. The food shock that drives it
also appears in the bottom-quintile consumption capacity output — the human cost the
financial figure represents."

**Intergenerational Advocate assessment:**

Reserve depletion to 2.0 months by step 5 under external conditionality is a path that,
if not reversed, produces irreversible capability losses in under five years: education
system funding cuts, maternal health infrastructure deterioration, nutritional stunting in
under-five cohort. None of this is named in the demo. The north star test for the demo
(can the minister argue something at the table that she could not argue before?) is met
by the trust architecture alone. But "what happens to children born in 2025 in Zambia if
this trajectory continues" is the answer to why that argument matters. Consider one
sentence at Frame E.

**Community Resilience assessment:**

A food price shock of magnitude 0.35 in a country with 64% rural population and high
subsistence agriculture penetration is not primarily a reserve story. It is a community
food security story. The demo scenario would benefit, even at the Q&A level, from one
prepared answer connecting the reserve mechanism to food security: "The import dependency
coefficient in the model captures exactly this — when the cost of food imports rises, it
depletes the foreign exchange reserves the ministry holds. The reserve is the financial
shadow of the food stress."

**Resolution:** Accept for Demo 5. File Issue for M15 scope: HCL narration integration
into the five-frame demo structure. North star test for M15 demo: "Can the minister
name the food security impact in the same sentence as the reserve figure?"

### Investment Agent — overall assessment

The PSP output (programme_survival_probability) is analytically differentiated from any
currently available public tool. The framing — "not whether the programme is approved,
but whether it can be implemented" — is precise and will resonate with institutional
funders who have seen approved programmes fail on implementation. The walkthrough narration
for Frame D is correct in spirit; once DEMO5-002 (WARNING/CRITICAL error) is fixed, the
Frame D narration is sound.

### Geopolitical Analyst — overall assessment

The challenge-response framing is accurate to the actual IMF programme review dynamic.
"The source is the IMF's own publication" is the sharpest line in the walkthrough — it
directly addresses the information asymmetry argument without being adversarial. Retain
verbatim. The scenario dynamics (food shock + conditionality) are consistent with the
ZMB 2024 ECF context. No geopolitical accuracy concerns.

### Social Dynamics — overall assessment

Legitimacy index 0.55 → IMF acceptance erosion → 0.45 (below fragility threshold 0.5)
producing FRAGILITY_AMPLIFIER=1.5 on PSP is a correct social dynamics sequence. The
walkthrough does not name this chain explicitly, which is appropriate for a non-technical
audience. However, if asked "why does programme survival probability matter alongside
reserve coverage?" the presenter's scripted answer ("whether the government can deliver
what the approval requires") is accurate.
