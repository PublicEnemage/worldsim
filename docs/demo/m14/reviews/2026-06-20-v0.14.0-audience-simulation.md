**[DEMO-097]: Presenter claims "self-interpreting sentence" that does not appear on screen**
Severity: CRITICAL
In-character observation: "The presenter says, twice — in Step 2 and again in Step 3 — 'the self-interpreting sentence: at the current draw rate, the CRITICAL floor is one step away.' I am looking at the screen. What I see is '16.3% above floor at step 3. 0 consecutive steps.' Those are not the same statement. One is a directional trajectory sentence I could put in a briefing note. The other is a static percentage with a step counter I do not know how to interpret. If the presenter is narrating text that is not on the screen, I now have to wonder what else in this demonstration is narration rather than output. That is the moment I stop listening."
Presenter preparation: "The walkthrough says Zone 1B displays 'at the current draw rate, the CRITICAL floor is one step away.' The screen shows '16.3% above floor at step 3. 0 consecutive steps.' Recommendation: Either (a) the Layer 3 sentence must be implemented and visible in the application, or (b) the walkthrough narration must be revised to describe only what the screen actually shows. The presenter cannot narrate output that does not exist. **This is the highest-severity finding: it occurs at the thesis moment and contradicts the demo's central capability claim.**
