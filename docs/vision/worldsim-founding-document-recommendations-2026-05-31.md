# Recommendations for the WorldSim Founding Document
*Additions arising from the May 2026 intellectual foundations conversations*
*For EL review and decision*

---

## Summary

The conversations documented in `worldsim-intellectual-foundations-2026-05-31.md` produced several ideas that warrant direct incorporation into the Founding Document. These are not peripheral additions. They resolve specific gaps in the document as it currently stands and strengthen its central claims.

The recommendations are organized in the order they would appear in the document, not by priority. All are recommended. The EL should decide which belong in the current document and which in a separate companion section.

---

## Recommendation 1: Add Part Two-A — The Ethical Foundation

**What:** Insert a new section between the current Part Two (The Analogy) and Part Three (The Principles), containing the six principles grounded in Islamic jurisprudence and Quranic imperative.

**Why:** The current Principles section articulates WorldSim's design commitments (multi-currency measurement, no false precision, defense not offense, backtesting as discipline, open source as strategy). These are operational principles. The Ethical Foundation is the moral grounding beneath them — the answer to the question "why does it matter that we get this right?" The six principles provide that grounding in a tradition that has been working on exactly these questions for fourteen centuries.

**Suggested preamble:**

> The ethical commitments of WorldSim find resonance in the tradition of Islamic jurisprudence — specifically in the five grand maxims of Shariah (Al-Qawa'id Al-Khams Al-Kubra) and in several Quranic imperatives concerning justice, trusteeship, and honest witness. These principles are not claims of Islamic authority for a secular analytical tool. They are an honest acknowledgment that the questions WorldSim addresses — how to protect the vulnerable from structural harm, how to hold analytical capability accountable to the people it affects, how to be a faithful witness to reality rather than a servant of power — are questions that Islamic jurisprudence has been working on for fourteen centuries, and that its answers are worth learning from.

**The six principles:** As drafted in the intellectual foundations document (Sections I–VI of Part One).

## Recommendation 2: Expand the Asymmetry Diagnosis in Part One

**What:** Extend the "The Asymmetry Is Not Just Informational" section to include the three-layered historical account:

1. The asymmetry was actively constructed over five centuries (Rodney)
2. The mechanism of construction was modernized — from military coercion to financial architecture — but the structural logic is identical (Perkins)
3. The movements to reverse it were suppressed, but the tradition of resistance continues (Prashad)
4. The interventions designed to address it risk reproducing it unless designed to restore capacity rather than substitute for it (Moyo)

**Why:** The current document says the asymmetry "is not accidental" and "is structural." This is accurate but incomplete. The fuller account — that it was engineered, that it replaced something that existed and was deliberately dismantled, that it continues through specific identifiable mechanisms — is both more honest and more powerful. It positions WorldSim not as charity but as a contribution to a fifty-year political project of analytical sovereignty restoration.

**Suggested addition:**

> This asymmetry was not assembled by accident. Walter Rodney documented its five-century construction: the productive and analytical capacity of the Global South was systematically extracted and its development deliberately suppressed as a precondition for maintaining extractive relationships. Colonial education produced clerks who could administer extraction, not economists who could model alternatives to it. The analytical gap that WorldSim addresses did not arise from differential talent or starting conditions. It was engineered.
>
> In the post-colonial period, the mechanism was modernized. John Perkins described from the inside how financial architecture replaced military occupation as the primary tool: debt relationships, structural adjustment conditions, manufactured urgency, information asymmetry maintained as a negotiating advantage. The actors changed. The structural logic did not.
>
> Vijay Prashad documented what was lost: the New International Economic Order of 1974, in which Global South countries collectively demanded the structural conditions that WorldSim is designed to partially provide — fairer terms, analytical sovereignty, reform of the institutional frameworks that maintain the asymmetry. That demand commanded a majority of the UN General Assembly. It was defeated by the Washington Consensus.
>
> WorldSim is not providing something that has never existed. It is restoring a capability that was deliberately removed. This framing matters, because it positions the project not as charity but as a contribution to a political project with a fifty-year history.

## Recommendation 3: Add the Multi-Spectrum Mirror to Part Two

**What:** Add a subsection to Part Two (The Analogy) extending the instrument cluster metaphor to the multi-spectrum mirror concept.

**Why:** The flight simulator analogy is powerful and should remain the primary analogy. But it has a specific limitation the document already names: it shows the pilot where they are, not what the structural forces are that constrain where they can go. The multi-spectrum mirror extension fills this gap — and the X-ray layer specifically addresses the structural dynamics that the optical layer conceals.

**Suggested section title:** "The Multi-Spectrum Mirror"

**Core addition:**

> The flight simulator analogy captures what WorldSim does for the finance minister in the room. But there is a second analogy that captures what it does for the community of practitioners beyond the room.
>
> WorldSim is a mirror. Not a flat mirror that returns the optical image — the GDP figures and fiscal balances that are presented as the complete picture. It is a mirror with toggles for non-visible wavelengths.
>
> The visible layer shows the standard macro indicators: real numbers, not fabricated, but curated for a particular narrative. The infrared layer shows the slow-moving human cost dynamics invisible in quarterly data — child malnutrition trends, life expectancy trajectories, the gradual erosion of healthcare system capacity. The ultraviolet layer shows early-warning stress signals — where the trajectory is heading before it visibly arrives. The X-ray layer shows the structural underpinnings: the propagation network of trade dependencies, debt relationships, and institutional constraints through which stress travels in ways invisible at the country level.
>
> The X-ray layer is the one that has been deliberately kept opaque. Not because the relationships are secret — they are documented in academic literature — but because making them operationally visible to the finance ministry during a negotiation changes the negotiating dynamic fundamentally. The structural layer is where the steel piles are. Illuminating it is WorldSim's long-horizon architectural commitment.

## Recommendation 4: Add the Principle of Instrument Coherence to Part Three

**What:** Add a seventh principle to the current five in Part Three (The Principles):

**The Principle of Instrument Coherence**

> No single instrument in WorldSim's cluster operates in isolation. A financial recovery indicator that contradicts a human development deterioration indicator does not resolve by choosing one reading to trust — it requires human judgment about which instrument is telling the true story and why. The multi-framework design is simultaneously a completeness mechanism and a verification mechanism: selective manipulation of any single output is made visible by its inconsistency with the others.
>
> And because the methodology is open, this verification extends beyond the negotiating room to the research community, civil society, and the public. One counter-analysis is an outlier. Twenty independently-generated findings that contradict the official analysis become a body of evidence that institutional authority cannot easily dismiss.
>
> A single instrument can fail — or be doctored. But no actor can simultaneously doctor the financial, human development, ecological, and governance instruments to tell a consistent false story. The inconsistency is the signal. The community is the verification mechanism.

**Why:** This principle directly addresses the Goodhart's Law risk the document already names, and extends it: the multi-framework design is not just completeness, it is the structural defense against manipulation. It also connects the instrument design to the network effect theory of change.

## Recommendation 5: Add Part Four-A — The Structural Theory and the Known Adversary

**What:** Add a new section between the current Part Four (The Architecture) and Part Five (The Vision), titled "The Known Adversary," that names the structural dynamics WorldSim is designed to work within and against.

**Why:** The current document is honest about technical limitations (Part Six). It is less explicit about structural adversaries — the forces that will resist adoption, adapt to close the gaps WorldSim opens, and maintain the asymmetry through new mechanisms as old ones are addressed. Naming the adversary is not paranoia. It is intellectual honesty, and it is the precondition for designing against it.

**Core content:**

> Immanuel Wallerstein's World Systems Theory provides the structural map of what WorldSim is working against. The core-periphery relationship in the global financial system is not maintained by bad actors. It is maintained by structural incentives — every actor in the system is rewarded for reproducing it, even without conspiratorial intent.
>
> The specific implication for WorldSim: the analytical asymmetry is not a contingent flaw in the system. It is a functional feature. The system requires that the finance ministry of a peripheral country not have the same analytical capability as the IMF in the same way that the system requires that commodity prices be set by the core. Closing the gap will generate structural resistance — new complexity in programme design, new information barriers, new conditionality requirements — because the system will adapt to maintain the advantage that WorldSim is designed to remove.
>
> This is not a counsel of despair. It is a design requirement. WorldSim must be built for adaptation, not for a one-time correction. The open-source commitment, the community of practice, the backtesting library that accumulates across cases — these are structural responses to a structural adversary. Not because any individual at the IMF is an enemy, but because the system they operate within will adapt, and WorldSim must adapt faster.
>
> The complementary tactical adversary is the negotiating playbook that exploits asymmetry in a room: anchoring, manufactured urgency, information asymmetry maintained as advantage, agenda overloading, bilateral isolation. These tactics are not unique to any single negotiating team — they are what power asymmetry makes available. WorldSim's instrument design addresses each of them specifically. The MDA threshold system neutralizes anchoring. The PMM makes the real BATNA visible. Mode 2 pre-run analysis defeats manufactured urgency. The open methodology defeats information asymmetry as strategy. The four-framework cluster defeats agenda overloading. The community of practice defeats bilateral isolation.

## Recommendation 6: Expand the Flywheel Section in Part Five to Include the Network Effect Theory of Change

**What:** Extend the current Flywheel section to incorporate the full network effect theory of change developed in the conversations.

**Why:** The current flywheel description focuses on individual learning — finance ministry analysts becoming better over time. The full theory of change is larger: it is about the emergence of a distributed accountability infrastructure that changes the conditions of all negotiations, not just individual ones.

**Suggested addition:**

> The flywheel is the individual learning cycle. The network effect is the collective one.
>
> Consider the Ground Penetrating Radar analogy. A single GPR unit produces a cross-section — useful, but interpretable only in isolation. A network of units, with a coordinated processing platform, produces a three-dimensional point cloud. The isolated anomaly becomes a data point in a pattern. The structural feature becomes visible as a structural feature precisely because the network has sufficient density to distinguish it from noise.
>
> One WorldSim deployment changes one negotiation at the margin. A network of deployments — with a shared backtesting library, coordinated case development, and dependency chain visualization built on the Wallerstein framework — produces the point cloud. The Greek fiscal multiplier anomaly is no longer an isolated finding. It is one data point in a pattern that includes Argentina, Lebanon, Thailand, Ecuador, and dozens of other cases. The structural dynamics that Wallerstein describes at the theoretical level become visible at the operational level.
>
> The mechanism of threshold change that Gandhi demonstrated in the Salt March applies here: the British system could absorb one person making salt. It could not absorb ten million. Structural power has a cost, and collective action raises that cost non-linearly. The analytical asymmetry is reproduced because doing so is cheaper than not doing so. A growing community of practice that consistently, publicly, and credibly challenges the official analysis changes that calculus.
>
> The Islamic concept of Jama'ah — community holding the rope together — provides the theological frame: no single section-holder can hold the whole rope. But when enough sections are held simultaneously, the rope has structural integrity that no individual could provide. The community of practice is the rope. WorldSim is the section each analyst holds.
>
> What is impossible for one becomes plausible for a few, possible for more, probable with just a few more than that. Make the tools available. Let the community do what no single actor can do alone.

## Recommendation 7: Expand the Honest Limitations Section to Name the Structural Constraint Explicitly

**What:** Add a subsection to Part Six (Honest Limitations) titled "The Structural Constraint."

**Why:** The current limitations are technical: WorldSim doesn't model individual behavior, real-time markets, or geopolitical constraints. There is a deeper limitation that the document doesn't name: the structural dynamics WorldSim is designed to address will adapt to resist it. Naming this is not pessimism — it is the intellectual honesty the document commits to, and it is the basis for designing the community of practice as a structural response.

**Suggested addition:**

> **The Structural Constraint**
>
> WorldSim addresses the analytical asymmetry at the level of the specific negotiation. It does not address the structural dynamics that reproduce the asymmetry.
>
> Wallerstein's framework implies that as WorldSim closes one manifestation of the gap, the system will develop new mechanisms to reraise the analytical bar. The tool faces not a static adversary but a self-reproducing system that will adapt. New complexity in programme design, new information barriers, new conditionality frameworks — these are the structural responses to any tool that successfully reduces the asymmetry.
>
> The honest response is that WorldSim cannot fix the structure. It can change the conditions of specific negotiations. It can build a community of practice that accumulates analytical sovereignty across cases. And it can make the structural dynamics visible to people who currently cannot see them — which is the precondition for any structural challenge, however long-horizon.
>
> The network effect described in Part Five is the project's best answer to the structural constraint. Not a claim to have solved it — but a design commitment to building the distributed infrastructure that makes it progressively more expensive to maintain.

## Recommendation 8: Add the Public Advocacy Use Cases to Part Five

**What:** Add a paragraph to Part Five (The Vision) explicitly naming the public advocacy use cases.

**Why:** The current Vision section focuses on the finance minister and the flywheel of improving analytical practice. The public advocacy dimension — journalists, civil society, parliamentary economists, community researchers, the children of the Indian farmers — is the layer of the network that makes the threshold dynamics possible. It deserves explicit naming.

**Suggested addition:**

> The finance minister in the negotiating room is the primary user. She is not the only one.
>
> The investigative journalist who runs the same scenario and publishes the gap between the official narrative and the structural analysis. The parliamentary economist who challenges the programme design with independently generated findings. The civil society organization that monitors implementation and compares the trajectory against what was predicted. The community researcher in Fez who maps the dependency chain that explains why a Moroccan cannot own property in his own city while a European can. The children of the Indian farmers who finally see the complete causal chain of what happened to their fathers.
>
> Each of these users holds a section of the rope. Each additional section strengthens the whole. The tool is designed for the negotiating room. The network effect operates through everyone who uses it beyond that room.

## On Sequencing

These eight recommendations vary in scope and urgency:

**Immediate additions** (strengthen the existing document's core argument): Recommendations 1 (Ethical Foundation), 4 (Instrument Coherence principle), and 8 (Public Advocacy use cases).

**Substantial additions** (require new sections but address real gaps): Recommendations 3 (Multi-Spectrum Mirror), 5 (Known Adversary), and 6 (Network Effect expansion).

**Expansions to existing sections** (require careful integration): Recommendations 2 (Asymmetry Diagnosis) and 7 (Structural Constraint).

None of these diminish what the document already says. They extend it in directions that the May 2026 conversations opened.
